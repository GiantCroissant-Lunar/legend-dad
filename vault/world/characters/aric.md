# Aric

## Overview

The Son. Twenty years after his father Kaelen left Thornwall, Aric is a sharp-eyed young man raised by the village elder and trained in tracking and herbalism by his mother's old apprentice. He has grown up in the long shadow of a legend — everyone in Thornwall has a story about Kaelen, and none of them match. Aric is skeptical, resourceful, and quietly angry. He does not want to be a hero. He wants answers: why his father left, whether the legends are true, and what is happening to the land that seems to be slowly unraveling.

## Backstory

Aric was two years old when Kaelen left. He has no memory of his father — only stories, each contradicting the last. The village elder raised him with discipline and kindness. His mother Lira returned intermittently for the first few years, then stopped coming entirely. At twelve, Aric discovered his father's old journal hidden in the militia barracks, full of sketches and half-formed theories about tremors beneath the Iron Peaks. At sixteen, he began having nightmares about a place he'd never been — a sealed cavern pulsing with light. By twenty-two, the tremors his father wrote about have returned, stronger. Strange creatures emerge from cracks in the earth. The old forest is dying. Aric decides to follow his father's path — not out of love, but out of necessity.

## Personality & Motivation

Where Kaelen is warm and impulsive, Aric is cool and methodical. He plans before acting, questions before trusting, and resents before forgiving. He is not cold — he cares deeply about Thornwall and its people — but he has learned to manage expectations. His primary motivation is survival: the land is breaking and someone needs to fix it. His deeper motivation is reckoning with his father's legacy — deciding whether Kaelen was a hero who made hard choices or a coward who dressed up running away as duty.

## Relationships

- [[Kaelen]] — his absent father, known only through contradictory stories
- [[Thornwall]] — his home, now threatened by the returning tremors
- [[Maren]] — childhood friend and fellow tracker, the only person he trusts fully

### portrait

16-bit pixel art, grayscale base with palette-swap tinting. Young man in his early twenties, lean build, dark hair tied back in a short tail. Angular face, sharp eyes with a guarded expression. Wears a tracker's outfit — dark leather vest over a linen shirt, fingerless gloves, a utility belt with herb pouches. No sword — carries a hunting knife and a collapsible shortbow. Cool gray-blue dawn lighting, suggesting someone who wakes before the village. A faint resemblance to Kaelen in the jawline, but the expression is entirely different.

### voice

Quiet tenor, precise diction. Speaks in short, complete sentences. Rarely raises his voice — when angry, he gets quieter and more clipped. Dry humor that borders on sarcasm, used as deflection. When genuinely surprised or moved, his careful composure breaks and a warmer, less guarded voice emerges briefly. No regional accent — he deliberately trained it out, wanting to sound different from Thornwall's militia storytellers.

### theme-music

Solo piano in C minor, sparse and deliberate. Each note is placed with intention — no flourishes, no wasted motion. A quiet string pad enters in the second phrase, adding tension without resolution. The melody descends where Kaelen's ascends, suggesting someone walking the same path in reverse. In the bridge, a fragment of Kaelen's guitar theme appears in the piano, transposed to minor — the son carrying the father's melody without knowing it.
