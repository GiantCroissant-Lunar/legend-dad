# Physician Wren

## Overview

Herbalist and physician of Hollow's Rest. A slight woman of indeterminate middle age with sun-browned skin, graying dark hair in a loose braid, and hands that are never still — always sorting, grinding, measuring, folding paper envelopes of dried herb. Wren came to Hollow's Rest three months before Aric arrives, from a collapsed coastal village that she does not discuss. She is the settlement's only trained physician, and the only person who seems to know which of the new, corruption-adjacent plant growths in the Whispering Woods are safe to use medicinally and which will kill you slowly.

## Backstory

Wren trained at a small physician's school on the coast — not the Starlight Academy, a smaller institution that did not survive the son's-era collapses — and spent her working life treating fishermen, farmers, and their children in a village north of Saltmere that lost two-thirds of its population in a single summer when a wave of crystal-adjacent illness swept through. She was the last physician standing when the village emptied out. She walked three weeks through the Whispering Woods with a pack of her most valuable books and one child — her apprentice, ten years old — and found Hollow's Rest by following a trail Torbin had left deliberately for people in her condition. The child apprentice is now fourteen and rarely leaves Wren's side.

## Personality & Motivation

Focused and gentle, with a dry quiet humor that emerges only when she is tired. Wren treats her patients one at a time, with full attention, and she does not hurry. She is brisk with malingerers and infinitely patient with the genuinely sick. Her primary motivation is keeping Hollow's Rest's people alive through the winters and the corruption-adjacent illnesses that have started to appear. Her deeper motivation is teaching her apprentice everything she knows while there is still time, because she has seen what happens when the last physician leaves without teaching anyone.

## Relationships

- [[Hollow's Rest]] — her home for three months and counting; she has started calling it that
- [[Torbin]] — the elder who welcomed her; they drink tea together once a week and discuss nothing in particular
- [[Whispering Woods]] — the source of her materia medica and, increasingly, her materia pathologica; she maps the corruption's advance through her plant stocks

## Creative Prompts

### portrait

16-bit pixel art, grayscale base with palette-swap tinting. A slight woman of indeterminate middle age, sun-browned skin, graying dark hair in a loose braid over one shoulder. A linen physician's apron over a wool dress, both in forest greens and browns with small herb stains. A leather belt with small pouches, each carefully labeled with stitched symbols. Reading spectacles on a cord. A small silver pin on her collar shaped like a sprig of some plant that does not quite exist. Hands always in motion — the portrait catches her mid-grinding with a small mortar and pestle.

### voice

Soft contralto, patient cadence, a slight coastal lilt. Speaks in full sentences but will go silent for long stretches while working. Uses a patient's name frequently while treating them — "Breathe out, Torbin. Good." — as a grounding technique. Occasionally sings under her breath in a language no one at Hollow's Rest recognizes.

### theme-music

Solo hammered dulcimer in D minor, played at a walking tempo. The melody is repetitive in a way that feels deliberate — the sound of a medicine being prepared the same way it has been prepared for generations. A second voice enters mid-piece: a child's alto humming a harmonizing line, slightly uncertain, learning. This is the apprentice. The hammered dulcimer continues steadily while the child's humming finds and loses its way. The music ends with the two voices resolving to unison.
