# Cael Vire

## Overview

Harbormaster of Saltmere Port. A broad-shouldered woman in her late forties (in Kaelen's era; late sixties by Aric's era) with weather-roughened skin, gray-streaked black hair tied back in a sailor's knot, and the still, measuring gaze of someone who has watched a harbor for long enough to read its moods at a glance. Cael keeps the port's logs, arbitrates dock disputes, signs or withholds mooring rights, and in her official capacity represents Saltmere's guild council to visitors. In her unofficial capacity — which matters more by the son's era — she quietly runs what remains of the port's honest trade, and an increasing amount of its dishonest trade, because the Vanguard's tightening grip on coastal commerce has made the distinction meaningless.

## Backstory

Cael was born in Saltmere to a family of coastal traders and apprenticed on her mother's two-masted cutter before she was ten. By twenty she captained her own boat; by thirty she sat on the guild council; at thirty-eight she was appointed harbormaster after her predecessor died in a storm, and she has held the post ever since. In Kaelen's era, she is ten years into the job and still has her predecessor's desk, his kettle, and his rules. She cooperated easily with the old Vanguard on patrol coordination and dock inspections. A year before Aric's quest begins, the current Vanguard command tried to install a liaison officer in her tower over her objection. She refused the order, received a formal reprimand, and has been officially on administrative probation ever since — which she ignores. Her son went out on a fishing boat two years ago and did not come back; the boat was found a week later with a crystalline film on its hull and no crew. She has said nothing publicly about this.

## Personality & Motivation

Cael is economical in speech and direct in her dealings. She does not flatter, apologize, or explain herself unless the explanation is useful to the person she is speaking with. She has a dry, quiet humor that surfaces with people she likes. With strangers, she is cordial and gives nothing away. She reads people by their hands first, their eyes second, and their words last. Her primary motivation is keeping Saltmere functional — feeding its people, keeping its boats in the water, maintaining the harbor as something worth being harbormaster of. Her deeper motivation, which she has not spoken aloud since her son's boat was found, is finding out what took him and whether it is the same thing that is silting up the shallows. She suspects it is. She has not forgiven anyone for it, including herself.

## Relationships

- [[Saltmere Port]] — her town and her jurisdiction; she refers to it as "the harbor" rather than "the town"
- [[Kaelen]] — in father's era, she knows him by reputation (Thornwall's hero-militia-man) but has never met; "Iron in the Tide" is their first meeting
- [[Aric]] — in son's era, she recognizes Kaelen's son at a glance and does not say so; she is still deciding whether to help him
- [[Brine]] — the Saltscale Inn keeper, one of the three people in the port she actually trusts
- [[Nella]] — dockside fishmonger; Cael buys her breakfast from Nella every day and has done so for twenty-five years

## Creative Prompts

### portrait

16-bit pixel art, grayscale base with palette-swap tinting. A broad-shouldered woman, salt-weathered skin, black hair shot through with gray and tied in a tight sailor's knot at the base of her neck. Deep-set eyes with a measuring gaze. Wears a navy blue harbormaster's coat over a heavy cream linen shirt, brass buttons tarnished green-black, a wide leather belt with a ring of keys and a small brass spyglass hanging from it. Hands visible, callused, resting on a log book. In the father's era: shorter hair, the coat newer, fewer lines at the eyes. In the son's era: the hair is gray, the coat patched at the shoulder, and there is a small silver pin on the lapel — a fishing boat in profile — that she has worn since her son's boat was found. Cold gray-blue overcast lighting, suggesting the perpetual harbor weather.

### voice

Low contralto with a smoker's rasp (she does not smoke; that is just her voice). Measured cadence. She chooses her words and will sometimes stop mid-sentence if she decides the sentence is not worth finishing. No regional accent — the harbormaster trains one out — but a faint sailor's rhythm in how she breathes. Her laugh is rare, short, and genuine when it happens. When she is angry, she speaks more slowly, not faster. When she is grieving, she speaks about ropes, timbers, and tides as if they were the thing that mattered.

### theme-music

Solo bowed double bass in B minor, slow, with a low-register melody that rises and falls like the tide line on a dock post. A pennywhistle enters in the second phrase with a counter-melody in a higher register — not the concertina of the port's public theme, but a thin, lonely whistle like a boatswain's pipe heard across water. The piece does not resolve; it ends on an unresolved fifth that waits. In the son's era, the pennywhistle drops out entirely and is replaced by long silences between bass phrases. The listener waits for the whistle to come back. It does not.
