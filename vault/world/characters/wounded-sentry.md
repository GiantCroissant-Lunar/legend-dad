# The Wounded Sentry

## Overview

A Vanguard soldier — one of the few survivors of a patrol that went into the Iron Peaks six months before Aric's quest and returned, in pieces, over the course of three weeks. The sentry's right arm is crystalline from the elbow down: a pale violet growth that has fused with his flesh and taken the shape of an arm without being one. He cannot use it. It does not hurt. He can still stand watch. The Vanguard has kept him on active duty at the Ashenford checkpoint partly because he volunteers for the watches no one else will take and partly because the command does not quite know what else to do with him.

## Backstory

The sentry was a junior corporal on the patrol that entered the sealed tunnels six months before Aric arrives. Six went in; two came back in the first week (raving, and died); one came back in the second (the sentry); three never came back at all. He does not remember what happened underground. He remembers going in with his squad. He remembers being alone on a slope above Ashenford with his right arm heavy and cold. He was carried into town by a Saltmere salt-trader who found him sitting on a rock and looking east toward the sea, saying nothing.

## Personality & Motivation

Quiet, calm, and unsettlingly present. The sentry speaks when spoken to, in short clear sentences, and volunteers nothing. He does not complain about his condition. He does not appear distressed by it. When he is alone on watch, he stares at the crystalline arm for long stretches; when someone approaches, he stops and looks at them. His primary motivation, as far as anyone can tell, is standing his watches. His deeper motivation, which he has not expressed to anyone, is that he has decided the arm is listening for something, and he is waiting to hear what it hears.

## Relationships

- [[Ashenford]] — the garrison he serves; his barracks bunk has a crystal-shaped indent in the wall where his arm rests when he sleeps
- [[Iron Vanguard]] — the institution that does not know what to do with him; he is polite to his officers
- [[The Missing Patrol]] — the three soldiers who did not come back; he refers to them by rank and name, never by memory

## Creative Prompts

### portrait

16-bit pixel art, grayscale base with palette-swap tinting. A young man in Vanguard gray service kit, standard steel half-plate. Short black hair, pale skin, a calm and slightly absent expression. Left side normal: gloved hand resting on the pommel of a standard-issue shortsword. Right side: the armor plate has been cut away below the shoulder, and from the elbow down his arm is a pale violet crystalline shape that roughly follows the original bone structure — a wrist, a hand, fingers — but is clearly mineral, faceted, and gives off a faint glow in low light. The crystalline arm is not grotesque; it is oddly beautiful, which is its own kind of wrong. Lighting is dim winter afternoon through the Ashenford canyon.

### voice

Light tenor, even volume, no inflection spikes. Speaks in short declarative sentences, often the minimum required to answer a question. Pauses before answering, not because he is thinking, but because he is listening to something else first. No tremor. No distress. The voice of someone who has decided that what happened to him is information rather than injury.

### theme-music

A high crystalline synthesizer tone in a key that doesn't quite fit anything else — F sharp — held as a drone underneath a solo flute melody that tries three times to resolve to a recognizable folk theme and fails each time. A distant snare drum cadence from the Ashenford garrison march is audible in the background, slightly out of time with the flute. The piece is short, doesn't loop cleanly, and leaves a small silence before restarting. The sentry's music is music that has learned not to finish its sentences.
