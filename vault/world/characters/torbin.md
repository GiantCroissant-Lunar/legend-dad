# Elder Torbin

## Overview

Founder and elder of Hollow's Rest, the hidden refugee settlement in the deep Whispering Woods. Torbin is a small, thin man in his mid-seventies, with close-cropped white hair, a weathered beard clipped short, and hands that have spent sixty years working timber, rope, and accounts. He served on Thornwall's village council for three decades as the junior member who kept the ledgers and drafted the official correspondence with the Vanguard and the coastal guilds. In the son's era, three years before Aric's quest begins, Torbin led seventeen families out of Thornwall and into the woods when the Vanguard checkpoint made free movement impossible and the village council refused to formally acknowledge what was happening to the land. He is not the strongest person in Hollow's Rest, or the most skilled, or the most charismatic. He is the person who said out loud what the rest would not.

## Backstory

Torbin was born in Thornwall and has never lived anywhere else until Hollow's Rest. He apprenticed with the village's woodwright as a young man, took over the workshop at thirty-five when his master retired, and was elected to the village council at forty for his careful record-keeping rather than any charisma. For thirty years, he drafted Thornwall's minutes, kept its tithe ledger, and corresponded in his small careful handwriting with every faction that had business with the village. He was married for forty-four years to a woman named Elen who taught the Thornwall children their letters; she died of a winter fever four years before Aric's quest. It was her death and the council's refusal, in the same season, to formally record that the northern farms had become uninhabitable that broke something in Torbin. He resigned from the council in the spring, spent the summer gathering the families who would listen to him, and walked into the woods on the first cold morning of autumn with seventeen households behind him.

## Personality & Motivation

Torbin is quiet, patient, and precise. He is not a talker; he listens. When he speaks in council — he still runs a council of sorts at Hollow's Rest, though it has no official charter — he waits until others have said what they needed to say, and then he summarizes what was said, fairly, and asks what the settlement should do. He has a slight tremor in his left hand that he controls by pressing it against his knee under the table. His primary motivation is keeping the fifty-plus people of Hollow's Rest alive, fed, and hidden — one winter at a time. His deeper motivation, which he has never named aloud, is that he was not brave enough to leave Thornwall while Elen was still alive, and he is determined not to be that kind of man again. He keeps her comb on his writing desk. He has not opened the sealed leather tube Kaelen left in the Thornwall ledger thirty years ago, and he knows he should.

## Relationships

- [[Hollow's Rest]] — the settlement he founded and continues to shepherd
- [[Thornwall]] — his birthplace and the village he resigned from; he still considers himself a citizen in some private way
- [[Aric]] — Torbin knew Aric as a child, taught him his letters alongside Elen, and recognizes him instantly
- [[Kaelen]] — Torbin corresponded with him twice before he left for the Iron Peaks thirty years ago; he has been holding one of Kaelen's letters since
- [[Maren]] — Maren's father was the forester who drew the maps that led Torbin to the Great Fall; Torbin holds Maren's father's work in something close to reverence

## Creative Prompts

### portrait

16-bit pixel art, grayscale base with palette-swap tinting. A small, thin old man with close-cropped white hair and a short clipped beard. Deep-set eyes, heavy lids, a long patient face with pronounced laugh lines at the mouth that have become neutral with age. Wears layered wool and linen in forest colors — a moss-green overcoat of patched homespun, a cream tunic underneath, trousers of undyed wool, leather shoes that have been resoled more than once. A pair of small brass reading spectacles hangs from a cord around his neck. His left hand rests against his knee; his right holds a wooden writing stylus. Expression is calm and attentive. Dappled green forest lighting — Torbin is always rendered in the filtered light of Hollow's Rest's intact canopy.

### voice

Quiet tenor, slightly dry from years of speaking only when necessary. Deliberate cadence. He begins most responses with a pause that lasts just a beat longer than feels comfortable — he is genuinely thinking, not performing consideration. He says "I think" rather than "I know" even when he knows. He uses the word "we" almost always; "I" is rare enough that when he uses it, the listener notices. No regional accent; his council training trained it out. When he is moved, his voice goes softer rather than louder, and he looks at his hands.

### theme-music

Solo wooden recorder in E minor, played at a walking tempo with no accompaniment. The melody is a folk line — the kind of tune a man might hum while tallying a ledger — unadorned, simple, and quietly repeating. It has the shape of a song that was originally a children's lullaby; listeners who have heard Thornwall's Market theme in the father's era will hear a phrase from that theme buried in the middle of Torbin's piece, transposed down and slowed. A second voice enters briefly in the second pass — an alto humming without words, half a measure behind the recorder, following it. This is Elen. She is not in the fiction; she is in the music. The recorder continues after the humming fades.
