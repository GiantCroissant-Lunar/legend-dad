# Scavenger Mott

## Overview

Hollow's Rest's supply runner. A wiry young man in his mid-twenties with reddish hair cut unevenly short, a long face, and a permanent smudge of road-dust across the bridge of his nose. Mott makes the three-day round trip out of the settlement roughly once every two weeks, salvaging what he can from abandoned farmsteads, trading quietly with Nella at Saltmere when a signal is right, and occasionally palming something off a Vanguard supply cart when the quartermaster looks the other way. He does not steal from people. He steals from institutions. He is very particular about the distinction.

## Backstory

Mott was born in Thornwall; his family was one of the seventeen that followed Torbin into the woods three years ago. He was twenty-two at the time. Of the five young adults who came with their families, he was the only one who volunteered to go back out on the roads. He has a knack for reading which farmsteads have been visited by the Vanguard and which have been forgotten, which signals mean a trader is willing to deal and which mean a trap, and which parts of the forest are safe to cross at which hours. He has been caught twice by the Vanguard. Both times he has talked his way out. He does not tell Torbin about the second time.

## Personality & Motivation

Fast-talking, quick-moving, and genuinely cheerful in a way that surprises people who expect a scavenger to be grim. Mott likes his work — not the stealing, but the knowing — and he likes bringing things back. He tells stories at the Hearth Circle after a successful run and edits the scary parts out. His primary motivation is keeping Hollow's Rest supplied; his deeper motivation is being useful in a way that proves (to himself, never out loud) that the young adults who came with their families made the right choice to follow the old man into the woods.

## Relationships

- [[Hollow's Rest]] — home, the only place he has ever chosen to come back to
- [[Torbin]] — the elder he lies to, gently, to keep him from worrying
- [[Nella]] — Saltmere Port fishmonger; one of his quiet trade contacts; she calls him "redhead" and never uses his name
- [[Ashenford]] — the Vanguard depot he steals from; he respects Berin without knowing why, and has, once, put a stolen supply back

## Creative Prompts

### portrait

16-bit pixel art, grayscale base with palette-swap tinting. A wiry young man in his mid-twenties. Red-brown hair cut uneven by his own hand; a long thin face with freckles scattered across the nose. Wears layered road clothes — a faded green tunic over a canvas shirt, brown breeches, well-patched knee-high leather boots. A light canvas pack on one shoulder with the flap loose for quick access. A small curved knife at his belt, a water skin, and a hand-bound notebook in a side pocket where he logs everything he brings back. A smudge of road-dust across his nose that is basically permanent.

### voice

Quick tenor, musical cadence, full of warmth. Talks with his hands; you can hear it even when you cannot see him. Uses road-trader's slang freely and explains it if asked. Tells stories well. Lies smoothly when necessary and is aware, always, that he is doing it. His real voice emerges at the Hearth Circle at night, when he is unwinding — it is slower and quieter than his road voice.

### theme-music

Fingerpicked acoustic guitar in G major, quick tempo, bright phrasing. The melody is a traveling song — the kind of tune a road-trader hums to keep pace. Hand drum enters in the second pass, adding a light driving rhythm. The piece is confident and forward-moving, but in the bridge, briefly, the key shifts to E minor and the drum drops out, leaving the guitar alone to play four quiet measures — the sound of Mott between Ashenford and the woods, when no one is watching. Then the drum comes back in and the major key returns. This is his road voice and his Hearth Circle voice in a single piece.
