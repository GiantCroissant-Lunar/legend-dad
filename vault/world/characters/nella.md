# Nella

## Overview

Dockside fishmonger at the foot of the Low Docks in Saltmere Port. A compact, weather-beaten woman in her fifties (seventies by Aric's era), always in the same oilcloth coat and rope-belted apron, Nella has sold dockside fish out of the same wooden stall for thirty years and, in the process, has become the port's unofficial rumor source. She knows what every boat brought in before the harbormaster's log is written. She knows who has not paid their mooring fee in three weeks and why. She has strong opinions about every captain in the harbor and she shares them freely with anyone buying breakfast.

## Backstory

Nella inherited the stall from her mother, who inherited it from her mother. The Low Docks fishmonger role has been in Nella's family for four generations and is not formally part of the port's guild charter — it is older than the charter and nobody has ever dared to try to regularize it. Nella is twice widowed, has three grown children who all left Saltmere for the coastal plain, and lives in two rooms above the stall's storage shed with a one-eyed cat named Ship. In the son's era, she sells far less fish (the catch has thinned), but she is still at the stall every dawn.

## Personality & Motivation

Sharp-tongued and warm-hearted in equal measure, Nella speaks her mind whether asked or not and considers it an act of generosity. She will answer a stranger's question with a question, size them up, and then decide how much of the truth to give them. Her primary motivation is keeping the stall running and Ship fed. Her deeper motivation is that she believes Saltmere's working-class are the port's actual backbone — not the guild, not the harbormaster, not the Vanguard — and she has made herself a small quiet center of that backbone by being where they buy their breakfast.

## Relationships

- [[Saltmere Port]] — the port she has watched from one stall for thirty years
- [[Cael Vire]] — Cael has bought her breakfast from Nella every day for twenty-five years; neither woman would describe this as friendship and both would be offended if the other ever did

## Creative Prompts

### portrait

16-bit pixel art, grayscale base with palette-swap tinting. Compact, weather-beaten woman with salt-gray hair tied under a blue kerchief. Squinting eyes from thirty years of dawn sun on water. Wears a black oilcloth coat over a rope-belted apron stained with fish scales and brine. Hands constantly in motion — gutting, scaling, wrapping. A small knife worn at the hip in a leather sheath, the handle worn smooth and pale by her thumb.

### voice

Sharp alto, brisk cadence, a chopped-off way of ending sentences that suggests she does not waste breath. Coastal accent that she does not hide. Laughs at her own jokes — which are often at the expense of captains, guild officers, and anyone wearing new boots. Has a habit of calling strangers "love" and regulars by their names; the distinction matters.

### theme-music

Hand drum and tin whistle in D minor, quick tempo, played as if by a street-corner busker. The melody is a simple street-cry set to music — the sound of a working waterfront. The piece is deliberately rough, with uneven phrasing and a drum hit that occasionally lands a fraction late. It is the sound of a stall that has been in the same spot since before anyone alive can remember.
