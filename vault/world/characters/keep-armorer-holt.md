# Keep Armorer Holt

## Overview

Master armorer of Lastwatch in the father's era. A broad, grizzled man in his fifties with iron-gray hair cropped short, forearms ridged with old hammer scars, and a habit of tapping the steel he is working with his knuckle to hear its tone. Holt has kept the keep's armory for twenty years — sharpening blades, refitting scale, rehafting tools for the four-man garrison and the warden, and maintaining the signal platform's pulley system from the inside. He is the keep's second most permanent fixture after Jessa Vale. He does not appear in the son's era; the armory is sealed behind crystal growth.

## Backstory

Holt learned his trade in a coastal foundry town and enlisted in the joint militia at twenty-five after a falling-out with his brother over the family forge. He served in three postings before requesting Lastwatch at thirty-five, and once he arrived, he did not request another. He is not married. He has two grown children he writes to monthly; their mother is a widow who runs an inn in a coastal plain village he visits once a year on leave. He believes — though he does not say so — that the joint militia's cooperative model is the finest institution he has ever belonged to, and that Lastwatch is its finest outpost.

## Personality & Motivation

Steady, practical, and warm in a gruff way. Holt greets travelers at the armory door with a quick assessment of their gear and an offer to fix whatever is broken in exchange for a reasonable fee — or nothing, if the traveler is obviously passing through on militia business. His primary motivation is keeping the garrison's kit in working order; his deeper motivation is the signal pulley. He has rebuilt it three times over twenty years and considers it his quiet contribution to whatever the keep is watching for. The day Kaelen passes through, Holt offers to sharpen Iron Dawn for free, as a courtesy. Kaelen accepts.

## Relationships

- [[Lastwatch]] — the keep and the armory; his quiet domain
- [[Jessa Vale]] — the warden he has served with for ten years; they take tea together every seventh day, a ritual he invented
- [[The Watch Holds]] — in this quest, he assists Kaelen with preparations and repairs for the cavern-breach mission

## Creative Prompts

### portrait

16-bit pixel art, grayscale base with palette-swap tinting. A broad man in his fifties with iron-gray hair clipped short and a heavy gray-black beard. Leather apron over a short-sleeved cream linen shirt, forearms bare and lined with pale hammer-scars. A heavy leather belt with a small hammer in a loop at the hip. Sooty hands resting on the shaft of a half-finished blade. Warm orange forge-light from behind him lights the edges of his silhouette; the rest of the portrait is in cooler stone-gray from the armory's north window.

### voice

Deep bass with a slight rumble, unhurried cadence. Has a habit of tapping on what he is talking about — a blade, a table, a pulley rope — while he explains it, the way a good teacher underlines words. Laughs loud and short. Swears rarely and creatively, always about inanimate objects, never about people.

### theme-music

Hammer-on-anvil rhythm in 4/4, opening with four strikes alone before a low wooden flute enters with a simple melody in D major — direct, warm, without ornamentation. A second hammer adds a counter-rhythm at the halfway mark. The piece is a craftsman's march: regular, confident, grounded in work. It ends on a single final hammer-strike at the end of the sixteenth bar.
