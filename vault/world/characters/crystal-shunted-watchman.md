# The Crystal-Shunted Watchman

## Overview

A figure Jessa Vale sees on the Lastwatch signal platform at irregular intervals in the son's era — always at dusk, always at the southwest edge of the platform, always looking at the Iron Peaks. He wears the joint-militia coat of a keep watchman from roughly thirty years ago, and his right shoulder and neck are fused with crystalline growth that extends down into the stone of the platform itself, as if he is part of the keep's fabric. He is not clearly alive, not clearly dead, and not clearly present. He does not speak. He does not appear to notice Jessa. When she approaches him, he is no longer there.

## Backstory

The watchman appears to be Tesh Hallam, a keep watchman who disappeared in a rockfall near Lastwatch forty years ago — the official record has him as killed-in-service, body unrecovered. Tesh was a young man at the time, serving his first posting; he had been at the keep for four months. What his appearance on the platform means is unclear. Jessa, who has kept the only continuous journal of these sightings, believes he began appearing roughly two years after the crystal corruption reached the keep's foundations. Whether he is a ghost, a memory the crystal is replaying, a warning, or something the keep itself is growing, she has not decided. She has sixty-three entries.

## Personality & Motivation

He has no personality that can be observed. He has no apparent motivation. Jessa notes, carefully, that he always looks south-southwest — toward the Iron Peaks' sealed caverns, and toward Ashenford beyond them. She notes, also carefully, that he appears only at dusk and only when the signal fire is being lit for the evening. She has twice seen him turn his head, very slowly, and look directly at the signal flame. The flame flickers when he does. Whether he is responding to the flame or whether the flame is responding to him, she has not decided.

## Relationships

- [[Lastwatch]] — the keep, to which he appears to be physically attached
- [[Jessa Vale]] — the warden who documents him; she says good evening to him, as a courtesy, when she lights the signal fire
- [[The Crystal Shadow]] — in Aric's quest, Aric sees the watchman once and Jessa tells him what she knows; the watchman does not acknowledge either of them

## Creative Prompts

### portrait

16-bit pixel art, grayscale base with palette-swap tinting. A young-looking man — early twenties in apparent age — wearing a dark blue joint-militia watchman's coat (the old cooperative design, three-flames sigil on the collar). His face is pale and composed, with no expression, looking away from the viewer to the south-southwest. His right shoulder and the right side of his neck are a pale violet crystalline growth that does not end at his skin — it extends down into the stone of the signal platform beneath him, fusing watchman and keep. His left hand rests on a signal-horn that is clearly the same signal-horn Jessa uses — or its copy. Dusk lighting, cold silver-blue, with a small warm point of orange from the signal fire at the edge of the frame. He does not cast a clear shadow.

### voice

He has no voice in the fiction. He has never spoken in any of Jessa's sixty-three entries. When he is approached, he is not there.

### theme-music

A single pitched crystal tone — not quite a note, closer to a sustained pure-tone oscillator — held for eight slow seconds before a second tone joins it a perfect fifth above. A third tone slowly phases in a quarter-tone off from the fifth, creating a faint audible beat. Underneath all of this, so quiet it may be imagined, a fragment of a joint-militia march that no one has played in thirty years — played backwards, very slowly, as if recalled rather than remembered. The piece has no rhythm. It has no resolution. It fades, slowly, over twelve seconds, leaving the listener unsure whether it was ever playing.
