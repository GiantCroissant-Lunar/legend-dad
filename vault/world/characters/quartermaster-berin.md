# Quartermaster Berin

## Overview

Quartermaster of the Ashenford Vanguard depot. A short, stocky man with a face like a disappointed pillow and a voice that has ground itself thin on fifteen years of daily inventory. Berin controls the flow of supplies into and out of Ashenford — food, medicine, weapons, cloth, tools — and in Kaelen's era he serves as the primary point of contact for civilian travelers who need to purchase Vanguard-released goods at the lowland rate. In Aric's era, he still holds the post, but his inventory is larger, his disbursements are smaller, and his temper is worse.

## Backstory

Berin enlisted in the Vanguard at nineteen because his family's coastal-plain farm could not support another son, and he discovered, to his own surprise, that he had an aptitude for accounts. He rose through the quartermaster corps and arrived at Ashenford as a subordinate clerk under Rhen Halloway. Within four years he was promoted to senior quartermaster. He served under Rhen for fifteen years and considered the captain the closest thing he had to a mentor. When Rhen turned in his commission six years before Aric's quest, Berin did not follow — he had a daughter to support and a pension to protect — and he has not forgiven himself for the choice.

## Personality & Motivation

Gruff, methodical, and quietly bitter. Berin treats everyone who comes to his depot with the same abrupt courtesy, civilian or soldier; the abruptness is procedural, not personal. He is scrupulously honest with his accounts and slightly less scrupulous with the paperwork around them — he has, on several occasions in the son's era, mis-filed a supply release in a way that let a refugee family receive something they would not otherwise have gotten. He does not call this generosity. He calls it "clerical error." His primary motivation is keeping the depot's books clean and its shelves stocked. His deeper motivation is private and connected to the captain who is not his captain anymore.

## Relationships

- [[Ashenford]] — the depot and the town; one and the same in his mind
- [[Iron Vanguard]] — the institution; he salutes the current banner without conviction
- [[Rhen Halloway]] — his former captain; they have not spoken in six years and both of them count the days

## Creative Prompts

### portrait

16-bit pixel art, grayscale base with palette-swap tinting. Short, stocky man in his late forties (mid-fifties in son's era). Pale blue Vanguard service coat with quartermaster's double-chevron on the sleeve, a heavy leather belt with an iron ring of inventory keys, a stub pencil tucked behind his ear. Thinning brown hair going gray. A face that looks mildly disappointed as its resting expression. Ink stains on his fingers.

### voice

Gravelly baritone that sounds like it has said "next" ten thousand times. Brisk cadence. Uses institutional phrasing by reflex ("requisition slip, three copies") but will slip into plain speech if the person in front of him seems worth the breath. Sighs before he says anything important, as if each important sentence costs him something.

### theme-music

Low brass (tuba or euphonium) in G minor, playing a slow, regular march-like pattern — the sound of an institution grinding on. A quiet tack-tack rhythm of a wooden pencil on a desk sits underneath, in steady 4/4 time. The melody is formal and repetitive, then briefly opens up in the middle for four bars into something warmer — a phrase that sounds borrowed from Rhen Halloway's theme — before returning to the institutional grind. Berin's music is Rhen's theme with a pencil on top of it.
