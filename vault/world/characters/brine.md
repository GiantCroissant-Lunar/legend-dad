# Brine

## Overview

Keeper of the Saltscale Inn, the main public house on Saltmere Port's waterfront. A stout, bald man in his sixties with a gray sailor's beard and a canvas apron over a homespun tunic, Brine is the port's unofficial news exchange, rumor clearing-house, and respite for anyone who crosses the waterfront at the wrong time of day. He serves the inn's function — rooms to rent, food to eat, a warm hearth in a port that does not offer many — and he offers visiting travelers the kind of information an innkeeper has that no one else quite has access to: who came in on the last tide, who has been drinking alone, which boats have not come back.

## Backstory

Brine sailed with three different coastal traders in his twenties before a leg injury put him ashore permanently. He took over the Saltscale from his father, who took it over from his mother, who built it with her husband the year Saltmere's guild charter was signed. The inn is eighty years old. Brine has run it for twenty-eight. He was never married; he had a partner named Onn who died of fever a decade before Kaelen's era, and the inn's back kitchen still has Onn's knife rack hung where it has always hung. Cael Vire eats her supper at the back table most nights. In the son's era, the inn serves fewer guests and much more cheap broth, but the lamps are still lit.

## Personality & Motivation

Warm without being effusive. Brine will remember a traveler's name and their preferences for ale after one visit and will use both on the second. He is a good listener — genuinely interested rather than transactional — and a cautious talker, weighing what to share with which audience. His primary motivation is keeping the inn a place where the working port's people can land at the end of a long day. His deeper motivation is giving Onn's kitchen back the busyness it had when they ran it together. He does not expect to reach it; he is not trying to.

## Creative Prompts

### portrait

16-bit pixel art, grayscale base with palette-swap tinting. A stout bald man in his sixties with a salt-gray sailor's beard trimmed close at the jaw. Broad shoulders, thick forearms, a slight limp visible when he moves (left leg). Wears a cream linen tunic with rolled sleeves, a canvas apron stained with grease and old wine, a cloth tied at the brow to keep sweat off when the inn is busy. A small tin whistle tucked in his apron pocket — he plays it sometimes when the inn is empty.

### voice

Warm bass voice with a coastal-trader's easy cadence. Laughs quickly and quietly at the small jokes travelers make. Uses nautical metaphors without self-consciousness ("bunk yourself down for the night, the tide won't rise till sunup"). Has a habit of ending a line with a small affirming grunt — "hm" — when he agrees with what was said.

### theme-music

Bright piece on concertina and tin whistle in G major, echoing the Saltmere Port public theme but at a more intimate volume — the sound of a single room at the end of a long day. A wooden spoon tapping on a mug provides the rhythm. The piece is short, loops cleanly, and has the feel of a song someone is humming to themselves while washing dishes.
