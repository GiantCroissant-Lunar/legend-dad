# Saltmere Harbor Docks

## Overview

The working waterfront of Saltmere Port — the zone that holds the harbormaster's tower, the Low Docks, Nella's fish stall, and the base of the two stone breakwaters. This is the player's primary hub in Saltmere in both eras, and the zone where most of "Iron in the Tide" and the opening beats of "Smuggler's Cove" take place.

## Layout & Terrain

Wide coastal zone (24x18 tiles) oriented with the sea to the east (right edge) and town buildings on the west. The zone is divided roughly into three north-south strips: the water (eastern third — walkable dock tiles extending into it), the dockside strip (middle third — flagstones, stalls, bollards), and the waterfront buildings (western third — warehouses, Harbormaster's Tower, Saltscale Inn facade partial). The Harbormaster's Tower is a 3x3 structure in the north with an entrance that transitions into its own interior zone (not covered here). The Low Docks extend from the southern half — five parallel wooden piers, each 2 tiles wide, reaching east into the harbor water. Nella's fish stall is a small 2x1 structure at the base of the central Low Dock pier. Exits: west (to Saltmere Merchant Quarter), south (to Saltmere Chalk Estuary via the coast road).

## Entities & Encounters

**Father's era:**
- Cael Vire on the third floor of the Harbormaster's Tower (accessible via tower door in the north)
- Brine visible at the Saltscale Inn facade door (transitions into inn interior)
- Nella at her fish stall (ambient dialogue, rumor source)
- 3-4 fisher NPCs working the Low Docks (ambient, mooring or unloading)
- 2 gulls (animated ambient sprites, ignorable)

**Son's era:**
- Cael Vire same position (warier greeting on first meeting)
- Brine same (quieter, recognizes Aric's eyes)
- Nella same stall (has more to say if asked about Kaelen's visit)
- 1-2 fisher NPCs (fewer boats, fewer hands)
- 3 boarded-up Low Dock slips (visual flavor, impassable end tiles)

## Era Variants

**Father → Son changes:**
- Low Docks: five piers, all active with moored boats → three piers functional, two rotted with planks missing
- Harbor water tile: clear teal-blue → muddy gray-green at the dockside edge
- Flagstone paving: clean, wet in places → cracked, salt-rimed
- Harbormaster's Tower pennant: crisp blue-and-white → frayed, the seaward beacon on the north breakwater now unlit
- Fish stall: full of fresh catch sprites → half-empty
- Warehouse walls: weathered silver but maintained → patched with mismatched salvage wood
- Gull count: 4-6 → 2

## Creative Prompts

### tilemap-art

16-bit pixel art tileset, top-down perspective, 16x16 tile grid. Core tiles: silver-gray weathered timber warehouse walls with rust-stained iron braces (west strip), flagstone paving tiles (middle strip, 2-3 weathered variants), sloping wooden dock tiles extending east (1-tile-wide plank patterns, walkable), rope bollard props at dock heads, stone breakwater tile (curves north and south at the edge of the zone — implied off-screen), harbor water tiles (teal-blue in father's era with white foam edge at dock lines, muddy gray-green in son's era), small fishing boat sprite overlays at moored slips, Harbormaster's Tower base (stone, 3x3, iron-banded door sprite facing south). Nella's fish stall: small wooden 2x1 with a red awning in father's era, awning stripped in son's era. Son's era additions: rotted dock variants with gap-toothed planks (impassable end tiles), abandoned boat hull props leaning against pilings, scum-crusted waterline tiles. Palette per location page.

### ambience

Father's era: full working-port soundscape — gulls calling overhead, wave-slap against pilings at slow intervals, rope creaking on bollards, ship bells at varied distances, a caulker's hammer, fishmonger calling prices, rope reels clacking, cart wheels on flagstone. Under it all, the low rush of surf on the breakwaters. Son's era: same bed, thinned — fewer gulls, no caulker, Nella's call heard once not repeated, surf on breakwater has a new hiss from sediment frothing against the stones, plus the slow grinding shift of silted mud when the tide pulls back.

### music

Shares the Saltmere Port location theme. Father's era: concertina and tin whistle in D major, moderate walking tempo, the lilting lead melody of the port. Son's era: same melody on concertina, slower, no whistle, a single bass drum at long intervals like a distant bell buoy, solo fiddle counter-melody in the relative minor. The theme plays at full volume in this zone — the Harbor Docks are the signature of Saltmere.
