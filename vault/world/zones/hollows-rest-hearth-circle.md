# Hollow's Rest Hearth Circle

## Overview

The central hub of Hollow's Rest — the natural amphitheater formed where three ancient trunks fell across one another a century ago and rotted down into a broad bowl of deep moss and rich loam. The Hearth Circle itself is a ring of river stones at the bowl's center with a shared cook-fire; around it are the settlement's fifty-odd inhabitants' dwellings, built into the root-arches of the fallen trunks without cutting the living canopy above. This is the zone where "Roots of Exile" climaxes and where "The Old Elder's Map" begins.

## Layout & Terrain

Circular-organic zone (18x16 tiles). The tree-fall bowl occupies the entire zone — three massive fallen trunks (each 4-5 tiles long, 2 tiles wide) overlap at the zone's west, north-northeast, and south-southeast edges, forming the natural walls of the amphitheater. The Hearth Circle at the exact center is a 3x3 ring of river stones with the cook-fire at the middle (1x1 impassable, walkable around). Root-arch cottages are built into the fallen trunks: Torbin's on the northern trunk (entrance facing south into the bowl), the Old Midwife's Hearth where Physician Wren works on the east side (entrance facing west), the Salvage Lean-To on the south side (open-sided), and three smaller family dwellings around the western arc. River-stone paths wind between the cottages through deep moss. The Listening Stones (three large stones) sit at the zone's southern edge, where children keep watch. Exits: south (to Hollow's Rest Hidden Path via a small gap between the southern trunk and the Listening Stones — walkable only if the player has been introduced to the settlement).

## Entities & Encounters

**Son's era only:**
- Torbin at the door of his cottage on the north trunk (quest-giver for "Roots of Exile" and "The Old Elder's Map")
- Physician Wren at the east cottage door (dialogue: supply list, herb trade, coastal-corruption questions)
- Scavenger Mott at the south Salvage Lean-To when not out on a run (roughly 50% availability; when present, dialogue about road conditions and Vanguard patrol schedules)
- Wren's young apprentice (14, non-named archetype, ambient near Wren's cottage)
- 2-3 child sprites at the Listening Stones (ambient watch duty)
- 2 hunter sprites at the northern edge (ambient sharpening arrows)
- 1 midwife sprite at the east cottage (ambient, unnamed)
- Shared cook-fire at the Hearth Circle (ambient hearth-crackle sound, warmth-glow sprite)

## Era Variants

This zone does not exist in the father's era. Hollow's Rest was founded three years before Aric's quest; the great fall happened a century ago but was left as impassable tangle until Torbin led the seventeen families into it. No LDtk era-variant toggle is needed — the zone simply does not render in the father's timeline.

## Creative Prompts

### tilemap-art

16-bit pixel art tileset, top-down perspective, 16x16 tile grid. Core tiles: massive fallen trunk tiles (4-5 tiles wide, 2 tiles deep, moss-covered brown bark with fern-sprite overlays, half-rotted and darker on the ground side), root-arch dwelling tiles (cottages built directly into root systems, with woven-wattle walls visible between exposed roots and living-root lintels above doors), deep-moss ground tiles (rich varied greens with occasional small white mushroom sprites), river-stone path tiles (flat gray stones pressed into moss, walkable), Hearth Circle stone-ring tile (a 3x3 arrangement with the central cook-fire sprite — warm orange glow, thin smoke wisp that disperses upward through the canopy), Listening Stones sprite (three large gray stones arranged in a loose triangle, 2x2 total footprint), Salvage Lean-To tile (open-sided wooden shed with stacked crates, barrels, and small iron-tool silhouettes inside), canopy overlay tile cast above the entire zone (semi-transparent dappled green-gold pattern layered above the ground, suggesting intact overhead canopy). The visual palette is the inverse of Ashenford's gray — rich greens, warm browns, muted ambers, the saturated earth tones of a settlement that has grown into its landscape rather than been built on top of it. Torbin's cottage is the largest and most obviously a root-arch dwelling; Wren's has small stitched herb-symbol sprites on the doorframe.

### ambience

A deliberately quieter soundscape than the rest of the son's-era woods. The Whispering Woods' canopy whisper is present at normal (pre-corruption) pitch — the old version of the forest's sound, rendered cleanly here because the corruption has not reached this pocket. Small wood fires crackle at low volume (mostly the central Hearth Circle fire; cottage hearths go cold after dark to keep the settlement invisible from above). A child's voice, hushed, carries briefly before a parent shushes it. Someone splitting kindling at slow intervals. A broom on packed earth. Wren's mortar-and-pestle grinding herbs. A hunter sharpening an arrowhead. Birds sing here — real ones, at normal pitch, not the wrong birdsong of the corruption zones — but softly. No loud voices. No hammering. No ringing bells. The absence of machine-sound is the signature of the place.

### music

Shares the Hollow's Rest location theme. Solo mandolin in G dorian, fingerstyle, walking tempo. The melody is a circular four-bar phrase repeating with minor variations — the sound of patience, of people who have decided to stay for as long as they can. A second voice enters in the second pass — a low-tuned whistle or recorder carrying a counter-melody in thirds underneath, suggesting quiet companionship. Near Torbin's cottage, his character's wooden-recorder theme layers in as a quiet third voice, and Elen's wordless alto humming briefly shadows the melody (from Torbin's character music). Near Wren's cottage, the hammered-dulcimer patient-medicine theme from Wren's character music replaces the second whistle. The piece drifts in and out of time with its own breathing — no percussion, no drone, a held breath slowly released.
