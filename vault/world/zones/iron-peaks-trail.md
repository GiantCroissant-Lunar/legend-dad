# Iron Peaks Trail

## Overview

The mountain trail ascending from the Whispering Woods tree line to the abandoned mine entrances of the Iron Peaks. A steep, winding path carved into the rock face with switchbacks and exposed ridgelines. Lookout Ridge — a flat outcrop with a panoramic view of Thornwall below — sits midway up. This zone transitions from forest to bare mountain, with increasing danger and decreasing vegetation as the player climbs.

## Layout & Terrain

Tall zone (20x24 tiles), oriented with the trail climbing from south to north. Southern quarter: tree line transition — scattered iron-bark pines on rocky ground. Middle: bare rock face with a switchback trail carved into it (2-tile wide path zigzagging up). Lookout Ridge is a flat 4x4 platform on the eastern edge at the midpoint. Northern quarter: the trail reaches a plateau with the mine entrance (a dark opening in the rock face, 2 tiles wide). Impassable: cliff edges (drop-offs), large boulders, sheer rock faces. The trail itself is narrow — limited room for combat, environmental hazards (loose rocks, narrow ledges).

## Entities & Encounters

**Father's era:**
- Mountain goats (passive, ambient)
- 1-2 hostile encounters (mountain bandits or rock creatures)
- Lookout Ridge: rest point, vista dialogue (Kaelen comments on Thornwall below)
- Mine entrance: locked/blocked, requires story progression to open

**Son's era:**
- Crystal-mutated creatures (tougher than forest encounters)
- Lookout Ridge: Aric comments on Thornwall's deterioration (the northern farms are visible as brown patches)
- Fissures in the rock face (new — glowing purple cracks, impassable but story-relevant)
- Mine entrance: partially collapsed but passable

## Era Variants

**Father → Son changes:**
- Iron-bark pines at tree line: green, alive → some petrified (stone-gray)
- Rock face: natural gray-brown → cracked with purple fissure lines
- Trail: intact with carved steps → some sections crumbled, alternate paths needed
- Lookout Ridge: clean stone platform → cracked, a fissure runs through the eastern edge
- Mine entrance: solid wooden door frame → collapsed timbers, dark opening
- Sky/background: clear mountain sky → slightly hazy, darker

## Creative Prompts

### tilemap-art

16-bit pixel art tileset, top-down perspective, 16x16 tile grid. Core tiles: rocky ground (gray-brown with rust-orange mineral streaks), carved trail tiles (smoother stone, lighter than surrounding rock, with step-edge details), cliff edge tiles (sharp drop-off shown as dark shadow gradient), iron-bark pine tiles (twisted dark trunk, compact green canopy — smaller than Whispering Woods trees), boulder tiles (large round rock, 1x1 and 2x2 variants), switchback turn tiles (trail bends, carved stone railing on outer edge), Lookout Ridge platform tiles (flat polished stone, lighter color, carved railing), mine entrance tiles (dark opening framed by timber supports, rusted iron rail tracks emerging). Son's era additions: petrified pine tiles (same shape but stone-gray with no green), fissure tiles (purple-violet line cracks in rock, emitting faint glow), crumbled trail tiles (broken edge, rubble), collapsed mine frame (fallen timbers, partially blocked opening). The zone should convey verticality — lighter tiles at top (sky exposure), darker at bottom (shadow). The trail should feel like a thin line of safety through hostile terrain.

### ambience

Father's era: wind dominance — constant, gusting, changing pitch with elevation. Rocky footsteps (hard, echoing against cliff faces). Gravel sliding underfoot on switchbacks. Eagle cry (distant, above). Iron-bark pine needles rustling (sparse, not like the forest's thick canopy). Occasional rock fall (small stones bouncing down). At Lookout Ridge: wind subsides slightly, distant Thornwall sounds barely audible (a hint of the village theme). Son's era: wind is stronger, with a whistling quality through the fissures. Crystal resonance from the fissures (a sustained, eerie tone that varies with wind). Rock fall sounds are more frequent and larger. Petrified pines make no sound (they're stone — the absence of rustling is noticeable). Creature sounds from the mine entrance area. At Lookout Ridge: a low rumble from within the mountain, felt more than heard.
