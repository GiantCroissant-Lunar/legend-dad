# Crystal Crawler

## Overview

The Crystal Crawler is an insectoid creature roughly the size of a large dog, with a segmented carapace studded with translucent crystal formations that glow faintly from within. It has six articulated legs ending in sharp crystalline points that allow it to cling to cave walls and ceilings with equal ease. Its mandibles are reinforced with the same mineral growths, capable of shearing through stone to reach the crystal veins it feeds on.

These creatures were completely unknown during Kaelen's era because they inhabited sealed cavern systems deep beneath the Iron Peaks -- chambers that no mine shaft had ever reached. When the land began to fracture in the years before Aric's story, cracks opened between these deep caverns and the existing mine network, releasing the crawlers into tunnels that humans had worked for generations. The first sightings were dismissed as miner's hallucinations until a surveying team was driven from a lower gallery by a swarm of glowing, clicking things that poured from a fissure in the wall.

Crystal Crawlers are drawn to concentrations of crystal energy, and the Iron Peaks' richest veins act as beacons that pull them upward through the fracture network. Their presence has made several of the deepest mine levels impassable, and the crystals they carry in their bodies interfere with the enchantments that kept the old mine supports stable.

## Ecology & Habitat

Crystal Crawlers are native to the lightless deep caverns beneath the Iron Peaks, ecosystems sustained by geothermal heat and mineral-rich water. In their native environment, they are part of a complex subterranean food chain, feeding on crystal growths and being preyed upon by larger deep-cave organisms that have not yet emerged through the fractures. Their crystal-studded carapaces are not armour but sensory organs -- each formation vibrates at frequencies that allow the crawler to navigate in total darkness and detect crystal deposits through solid rock.

Since reaching the mine tunnels, crawlers have established hive-like colonies near major crystal veins, constructing lattice structures from crystalline secretions. These hive walls resonate with a low harmonic hum that miners report hearing from several tunnels away. The colonies grow rapidly, and crawlers have been observed carrying crystal fragments back to the hive in a behaviour resembling resource stockpiling, though what they are building toward remains unknown.

## Behavior

In JRPG encounters, Crystal Crawlers appear in groups of three to six, often emerging from walls or ceilings to surround the party. They are fast and evasive, favouring hit-and-run tactics. Their basic attack, **Crystal Slash**, deals physical damage with a chance to inflict Crystallize -- a status that slowly reduces the target's speed as mineral growths spread across their armour.

Their signature group ability is **Resonance Pulse**: when three or more crawlers are present, they synchronize their crystal growths to emit a burst of disorienting energy that deals magical damage to the entire party and can cause Confusion. Individually fragile, their danger lies in numbers and coordination. A rare elite variant, the **Prism Crawler**, has fully developed crystal formations that refract magical attacks, granting it partial spell reflection.

## Lore & Cultural Significance

The miners of Iron Peaks had old superstitions about "the deep glow" -- a faint light sometimes seen at the bottom of the deepest shafts, always just beyond the reach of lantern-light. Elders warned that the glow was the earth's own fire and that digging toward it would wake things better left sleeping. These warnings were largely dismissed as the talk of men who had spent too long underground, but the emergence of the Crystal Crawlers has given the old stories a grim validation.

The crystals embedded in the crawlers' bodies have unusual properties -- they hold and release energy in patterns that some scholars believe resemble a primitive form of memory. A theory has emerged among Aric's contemporaries that the sealed caverns were not naturally formed but were deliberately closed off in an age before recorded history, and that the Crystal Crawlers are not wild animals but something closer to sentinels whose containment has finally failed. What they were meant to guard -- or guard against -- is a question with no comfortable answers.
