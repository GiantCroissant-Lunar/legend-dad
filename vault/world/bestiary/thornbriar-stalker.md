# Thornbriar Stalker

## Overview

The Thornbriar Stalker is a lean, wolf-like predator distinguished by the bony thorn-shaped growths that sprout along its spine and shoulders. Its coarse fur shifts between grey-green and dark brown depending on the season, providing natural camouflage within the dense thornbriar hedge that encircles Thornwall. Adults stand roughly hip-height to a grown man, with elongated limbs built for bursts of speed through tangled undergrowth.

In Kaelen's era, Thornbriar Stalkers were a rare but known hazard. Solitary hunters or mated pairs would occasionally drag off livestock that strayed too close to the hedge, and the town guard kept their numbers in check with seasonal culls. They were respected as dangerous but manageable -- a natural part of the thornbriar ecosystem that kept smaller vermin in balance.

By Aric's time, everything has changed. The hedge itself is weakening, its ancient root network destabilized by the fracturing of the land. Stalker packs have swelled to eight or ten members, emboldened by the thinning barrier between wild and settled land. They now hunt in coordinated groups, flanking prey with unsettling intelligence, and have begun venturing into the outer streets of Thornwall at dusk.

## Ecology & Habitat

Thornbriar Stalkers make their dens in hollowed-out sections of the thornbriar hedge itself, weaving shed thorns and undergrowth into nest-like structures. The thorn growths on their bodies are not merely decorative -- they allow the stalkers to push through the dense hedge without injury, a trait no other predator in the region shares. Their primary prey consists of hedge-rabbits, ground birds, and the occasional feral goat, though they are opportunistic and will scavenge from refuse pits near Thornwall's outer wall.

They are most active at dawn and dusk when the light filtering through the hedge creates deep shadows that favour their mottled colouring. In the Son's era, as the hedge grows patchier, stalkers have been spotted hunting in the open fields between Thornwall and the Whispering Woods -- territory they never ventured into before.

## Behavior

In JRPG encounters, Thornbriar Stalkers appear in groups of two to four. They favour a flanking strategy: one stalker will hold the party's attention with snarling feints while others circle to strike from behind. Their signature ability, **Thorn Lunge**, deals physical damage and applies a bleed effect from the barbed growths along their forelegs.

At low health, a stalker may use **Hedge Call**, a howl that has a chance to summon one additional stalker to the fight. In the Son's era, an alpha variant called the **Thornbriar Packmother** can appear as a mini-boss, commanding a full pack and using **Briar Cage** to restrict party movement by calling thorned roots from the ground.

## Lore & Cultural Significance

Thornwall's founders planted the great hedge as a living wall against the dangers of the wild lands, and the stalkers evolved alongside it over centuries. Local folklore holds that the stalkers are the hedge's own guardians -- that they keep the barrier healthy by pruning dead growth and driving out burrowing pests. The town's oldest families leave cuts of meat at the hedge-line during the winter solstice, a tradition called the Thornkeeper's Tithe.

This belief has made the stalkers' aggression in Aric's era deeply unsettling to the people of Thornwall. If the hedge's own guardians are turning hostile, many take it as a sign that the land's ancient compact with civilization is breaking down. Some desperate voices in the town council have proposed burning sections of the hedge to deny the stalkers cover, a suggestion that horrifies traditionalists who believe the hedge is all that stands between Thornwall and worse things in the wild.
