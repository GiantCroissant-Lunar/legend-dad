# Moss Lurker

## Overview

The Moss Lurker is a broad, flat-bodied ambush predator that inhabits the damp forest floor of the Whispering Woods. At rest, it is virtually indistinguishable from a mossy fallen log -- its segmented back is covered in a thick layer of living moss, lichen, and small ferns that grow directly from its hide. Only the faint rhythmic rise and fall of its breathing might betray its presence to a sharp-eyed traveller.

Beneath the camouflage lies a powerful creature roughly two metres long, with six squat legs ending in root-like claws that anchor it to the forest floor. Its head is little more than a wide, hinged jaw lined with rows of flat crushing teeth, designed to seize and hold rather than tear. When prey -- typically deer, boar, or an unwary human -- steps within striking distance, the Moss Lurker erupts from the ground with shocking speed, clamping down and dragging its victim to the earth.

The creature exists in both eras, though woodcutters and hunters have always known to watch for them. In Kaelen's time, experienced foresters could identify a lurker by the unnatural regularity of its shape and the absence of insects on its surface. By Aric's era, the Whispering Woods have grown denser and darker, and Moss Lurkers have spread into areas closer to the forest edge where they were never seen before.

## Ecology & Habitat

Moss Lurkers thrive in areas of the Whispering Woods where the canopy is thick enough to maintain constant dampness -- the moisture is essential for the symbiotic moss growth that provides their camouflage. They prefer positions along game trails and near streams where prey animals are likely to pass. A lurker will occupy the same ambush site for weeks at a time, only relocating when prey becomes scarce or the seasons shift.

They reproduce by budding: a mature lurker will shed a section of its back moss containing larval offspring, which take root in the soil and grow over several months into juvenile lurkers no larger than a boot. These juveniles are voracious, feeding on insects and small rodents until they reach sufficient size to tackle larger prey. The Whispering Woods' increasingly unstable ecosystem in the Son's era has accelerated their breeding cycle, leading to a noticeable population boom.

## Behavior

In JRPG encounters, Moss Lurkers always open combat with a **Surprise Ambush** -- unless the party has a detection ability or item active, the lurker gets a guaranteed first strike dealing heavy physical damage to a single target. Its primary attack, **Root Clamp**, deals moderate damage and has a chance to inflict the Immobilized status, preventing the target from fleeing or repositioning.

Moss Lurkers have high physical defence thanks to their dense hide and moss covering, but are weak to fire. Their ability **Moss Shroud** can raise their evasion for several turns as they partially re-camouflage during combat. When defeated, they sometimes drop Lurker Moss, an alchemy ingredient used in camouflage potions and healing poultices.

## Lore & Cultural Significance

The people of the Whispering Woods region have a saying: "Trust no log that has no rot." It is one of the first lessons taught to children who live near the forest. Woodcutters traditionally carry a long iron-tipped prod called a lurker-stick, used to poke any suspicious ground cover before stepping near it. The practice is so ingrained that "prodding for lurkers" has become a local idiom meaning to check for hidden problems before committing to a course of action.

In older folklore, Moss Lurkers are said to be fragments of a great forest spirit that shattered long ago when the first mines were dug in the Iron Peaks. Each lurker carries a sliver of the spirit's patience and hunger. Whether this has any connection to the land's current fracturing is a question that haunts the scholars who still study the old stories. Some believe the lurkers' spread toward settled areas is not mere ecological pressure but a slow, instinctive migration toward something they are trying to reassemble.
