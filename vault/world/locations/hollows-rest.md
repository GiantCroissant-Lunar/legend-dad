# Hollow's Rest

## Overview

A hidden settlement deep in the Whispering Woods, built in the hollow of a massive ancient tree-fall where three of the forest's oldest trunks toppled across one another generations ago. Hollow's Rest does not exist in Kaelen's era — the woods were too dense and too healthy to need a refuge, and the great fall had not yet formed. In Aric's era, as Thornwall and the smaller forest hamlets emptied under the tremors and the crystal corruption, a handful of families followed Torbin — once a junior on the Thornwall council — into the woods to build a new life off the roads the Vanguard controlled. The settlement is small, perhaps fifty people, and determined not to be found. It is three days' walk from Thornwall by a route only foresters know. Maren has known about it for a year and has said nothing to anyone.

## Atmosphere & Appearance

Hollow's Rest feels like a village grown out of the forest rather than built on top of it. The tree-fall hollow is a natural amphitheater: three ancient trunks, each wide as a cottage, collapsed across one another a century ago and rotted down into a broad bowl of deep moss and rich loam, ringed by the standing forest. The settlers have built into this bowl without cutting the living trees — low-roofed cottages of salvaged timber and woven wattle nestled between root-arches, paths of flat stone pressed into moss, a shared cook-hearth at the center in a circle of river stones. The canopy overhead is still mostly intact in this pocket — the crystal corruption has not reached this section of the woods, for reasons no one understands. Light falls in dappled greens. Woodsmoke rises gently through the canopy and disperses before it can be seen from outside. In the son's era, with so much else dying, Hollow's Rest feels strangely alive — not thriving, but persistent, a lung still breathing in a body that is failing.

## History

The three ancient trunks fell in a single storm about a century ago. Foresters marked the site on old maps as the Great Fall and left it alone; the tangle was impassable and the wood already claimed by rot. Three years before Aric's quest, as the tremors worsened and the Vanguard's checkpoint at Ashenford made movement between the lowlands and the mining settlements effectively impossible, small groups began to slip away from Thornwall and the outlying hamlets, trying to live at the forest's edge. Torbin, then seventy and newly widowed, led one such group. He remembered an old forester's song that described a safe hollow in the deep woods, and — guided by Maren's father's maps in his last year — found the Great Fall. The settlement is two years old. It has one midwife, two skilled hunters, a former blacksmith who shoes horses when anyone has them, and Physician Wren, who came three months ago from a collapsed coastal village and whose skill with herbs has saved more than one life already.

## Notable Features

- The Hearth Circle — a ring of river stones at the center of the hollow with a shared cook-fire. The only fire in Hollow's Rest at night; all smaller hearths go cold after dark to keep the settlement invisible from above.
- Torbin's Cottage — the largest of the dwellings, built into the root-arch of the northern trunk. Contains the settlement's shared records: who is owed what, who is sick, who has gone out and not come back. Also contains a sealed leather tube that Torbin has not opened.
- The Old Midwife's Hearth — a smaller cottage on the east side of the hollow where the settlement's midwife and Physician Wren keep the herb stores and tend the sick.
- The Salvage Lean-To — an open-sided shed where Scavenger Mott stores what he brings back from the roads: Vanguard-dropped supplies, abandoned farm tools, salt, nails. A rough ledger tracks what is owed.
- The Listening Stones — a cluster of three large stones at the hollow's southern edge that catch the wind differently and, the settlers swear, give early warning when something large moves through the forest toward them. Children are sent here to keep watch during the day.
- The Hidden Path — the only access to Hollow's Rest, a route that Maren can lead someone along but that is invisible to anyone else. It winds through swampy low ground, uses a fallen log as a bridge, and crosses a stream at a point where footprints don't hold.

## Creative Prompts

### environment-art

16-bit pixel art tileset, top-down perspective. Deep-forest-settlement tiles: massive fallen trunk tiles (each 4-5 tiles wide, moss-covered, with ferns growing from the rotting bark), root-arch dwelling tiles (cottages built into root systems, with woven-wattle walls and living-root lintels), deep-moss ground tiles in varied greens, river-stone path tiles laid into moss, a shared central hearth tile with a low stone ring and embers visible. Canopy overlay tiles cast dappled green-gold shadow across the ground. Wattle-and-daub cottage walls with small unshuttered windows. No banners, no signs — the settlement is unmarked. Smoke wisps from the central hearth, thin and dispersing into canopy. The edges of the settlement fade into standing forest via transitional tiles (thick undergrowth, fallen logs, low mist). Children sprites, two hunter sprites, the elder at his cottage door. Palette: rich greens, warm browns, muted ambers, low-saturation earth tones — the visual opposite of Ashenford's military gray.

### ambience

A deliberately quieter soundscape than the rest of the son's-era woods. The settlement exists in a pocket of still-healthy forest, so the familiar whispering of the Whispering Woods is present but at normal pitch — not the higher-pitched urgent whisper that marks the corruption zones. Small wood fires crackle at low volume. A child's voice, hushed, carries briefly before a parent shushes it. Someone splitting kindling at slow intervals. A broom on packed earth. The midwife's mortar grinding herbs. A hunter sharpening an arrowhead. Birds still sing here — real ones, not absent like the corruption zones — but softly, as if they too have learned to keep down. No loud voices. No hammering. No ringing bells. The absence of machine-sound is the signature of the place: Hollow's Rest is the sound of people living at the same volume as the forest around them.

### music

Solo mandolin in G dorian mode, played fingerstyle at a walking tempo. The melody is circular and contemplative, built around a simple four-bar phrase that repeats with minor variations — the sound of patience, of people who have decided to stay in one place for as long as they can. A second voice enters in the second pass, a low-tuned whistle or recorder carrying a counter-melody in thirds underneath the mandolin, suggesting quiet companionship. There is no percussion and no drone; the piece drifts in and out of time with its own breathing. Where Thornwall's folk theme in the son's era is lonely and the Vanguard march in Ashenford is oppressive, Hollow's Rest is neither. It is a piece of music that sounds like a deep breath, held, and slowly released.
