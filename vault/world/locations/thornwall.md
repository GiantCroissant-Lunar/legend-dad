# Thornwall

## Overview

A fortified farming village at the base of the Iron Peaks, named for the centuries-old thornbriar hedge that forms its outer wall. Thornwall is the starting location for both the father and son's stories. In Kaelen's era, it is a modest but thriving settlement of about two hundred people — a militia outpost, a market square, and farmland stretching south. In Aric's era twenty years later, Thornwall has contracted: the northern farms are abandoned, the thornbriar wall is patchy and dying, and the population has halved as families fled the worsening tremors.

## Atmosphere & Appearance

In the father's era, Thornwall feels safe and lived-in. The thornbriar hedge is dense and green, cottages have warm hearth-light, children play near the well, and the militia practices sword forms in the square. The Iron Peaks loom to the north, snow-capped and distant. In the son's era, the same village feels hollowed out. The hedge has gaps where the briars have rotted. Abandoned cottages are boarded up. The well water tastes of iron. The militia barracks is half-empty. The Iron Peaks seem closer — their silhouette darker, as if the mountains have leaned in.

## History

Thornwall was founded three generations ago by miners who abandoned the Iron Peaks after the deep tunnels became unstable. They settled at the mountain's base and turned to farming, using the natural thornbriar growth as a defensive perimeter. The village grew slowly, serving as a waypoint between the coastal trade routes and the mountain passes. Kaelen's militia heroics put Thornwall on the map; traders started making detours to visit "the hero's village." After Kaelen left, the village's fame faded, and the returning tremors drove away those who had come for the legend.

## Notable Features

- The Thornbriar Wall — a living hedge of ancient thornbriar, 10 feet tall and 4 feet thick, encircling the village. In father's era: dense, flowering. In son's era: patchy, with dead sections.
- The Market Square — center of village life. In father's era: busy with traders. In son's era: mostly empty, used for militia drills.
- The Militia Barracks — where Kaelen trained. Contains his old journal (found by Aric at age twelve). In son's era: half the bunks are empty.
- The Elder's Cottage — home of the village elder who raised Aric. Contains maps and records of Thornwall's history.
- The Northern Farms — fertile land stretching toward the Iron Peaks. In father's era: golden wheat fields. In son's era: fallow, cracked earth, and sinkholes.

## Creative Prompts

### environment-art

16-bit pixel art tileset, top-down perspective. Father's era: warm earth tones (golden wheat, green thornbriar, warm brown cottages with orange hearth-glow in windows). Cobblestone paths between buildings, a central well with a stone rim. Market stalls with colored awnings. Children sprites near the well. Militia guards at the gate. The thornbriar wall is a thick, flowering green border. Mountains visible in the background tile, snow-capped and distant. Son's era: same layout but desaturated — wheat fields replaced with cracked brown earth, thornbriar wall has gray dead sections, boarded-up cottages with dark windows, fewer NPC sprites, the well has a rusty bucket. Mountains in background are darker, closer-feeling.

### ambience

Father's era: birdsong, distant hammer on anvil, children laughing, wind through wheat, occasional cow lowing, merchant calling prices. A warm, alive soundscape. Son's era: wind through gaps in the thornbriar, a single crow cawing, distant rumble from the mountains (subtle, irregular), creaking of boarded windows, dripping water. The same base sounds but thinned out — fewer layers, more silence between events. Both eras share the sound of the well's bucket chain.

### music

Father's era: folk ensemble in D major — acoustic guitar, pennywhistle, and hand drum. Upbeat tempo, communal feel, like a village gathering song. Melodic and hummable. Son's era: same melody transposed to D minor, played on solo acoustic guitar with no accompaniment. Slower tempo, more space between notes. The familiar tune made lonely. A faint, low string drone appears in the second half, suggesting the distant tremors beneath the earth.
