# Whispering Woods

## Overview

A dense old-growth forest stretching between Thornwall and the coastal regions. The woods are named for the constant rustling of their canopy, which locals say sounds like murmured conversation. In Kaelen's era, the Whispering Woods are healthy and navigable — a source of timber, game, and medicinal herbs. Maren's family has patrolled these woods for generations. In Aric's era, the forest is dying from the inside out. Trees rot while standing, animal populations have collapsed, and sinkholes open without warning. The whispering has changed — it now sounds less like conversation and more like warning.

## Atmosphere & Appearance

Multi-layered canopy forest with massive trunks and filtered green light. The forest floor is soft with moss and fallen leaves. Streams thread between root systems. In the father's era: lush, vibrant greens, dappled golden sunlight, wildflowers along deer trails, bird nests visible in lower branches. In the son's era: the canopy has thinned, letting in harsh white light. Bark peels from trunks revealing gray wood underneath. Streams run slower and cloudier. The moss has patches of black fungal growth. Sinkholes are visible as dark circles in the forest floor, some ringed with crystalline growth similar to the Iron Peaks fissures.

## History

The Whispering Woods predate Thornwall by centuries. Local lore holds that the trees were planted by a civilization that existed before the current age — the roots go impossibly deep, and the oldest trunks are wider than cottages. The foresters who tend the woods have found carved stones deep in the undergrowth, suggesting ancient structures now buried beneath root systems. Maren's father discovered that the sinkholes appearing in the son's era follow the pattern of these buried structures, as if something below is pushing upward through the ancient foundations.

## Notable Features

- The Canopy Trail — an elevated path through the treetops, maintained by foresters. In father's era: a safe travel route. In son's era: sections have collapsed where supporting trees died.
- The Moss Clearing — a natural amphitheater where the forest floor dips and moss grows thick. Used by foresters as a campsite. In son's era: a sinkhole has opened at its center.
- The Ancient Stones — carved stone markers half-buried in roots, inscribed with symbols no one can read. Found throughout the deep woods.
- The Forester's Cabin — Maren's family home, at the forest's edge near Thornwall. In father's era: well-maintained. In son's era: Maren lives here alone, maintaining it stubbornly.
- The Deep Root — the oldest tree in the forest, its trunk thirty feet across. Local legend says its roots reach underground water. In son's era: still alive but visibly stressed, its bark cracked and weeping sap.

## Creative Prompts

### environment-art

16-bit pixel art tileset, top-down perspective. Dense forest tiles: massive brown tree trunks (some 3-4 tiles wide for the ancient trees), green canopy overlay that partially obscures the ground layer, moss-covered ground tiles in varied greens, stream tiles (clear blue in father's era, gray-brown in son's era), wildflower accent tiles (father's era only). Path tiles: dirt trails through undergrowth, elevated wooden walkway tiles for the canopy trail. Son's era additions: dead tree tiles (gray bark, bare branches), sinkhole tiles (dark circular holes with crumbled edges), black fungus overlay on moss tiles, crystal growth tiles near sinkholes (same pale violet as Iron Peaks). The forest should feel dense — limited visibility, lots of layered tiles creating depth. Father's era palette: rich greens, warm browns, golden light. Son's era palette: muted greens, gray-browns, cool white light.

### ambience

Father's era: a rich, layered forest soundscape. Wind through leaves creating the signature "whispering" — a constant, organic murmur. Bird calls (varied species at different distances), stream babbling, insect buzz, wood creaking, occasional animal movement in undergrowth. Footsteps on soft ground. Son's era: the whispering is still present but higher-pitched, more urgent. Fewer bird calls — long silences between them. The stream sound is thinner. Add: wood cracking and groaning (dying trees), a low subsonic rumble near sinkholes, crystal resonance chiming near fungal growth patches. The absence of the full animal chorus should feel wrong — a forest that has lost its voice.

### music

Father's era: layered woodwinds — two recorders playing in thirds over a finger-picked lute in E minor. The arrangement is gentle and cyclical, like walking in circles through familiar paths. Natural forest sounds bleed into the music — the boundary between soundtrack and ambience is deliberately blurred. Son's era: solo recorder playing the same melody, but with longer pauses and slower tempo. The lute is replaced by a low, bowed psaltery that adds tension. The melody occasionally breaks into dissonance — a wrong note, quickly corrected — suggesting the forest's corruption bleeding into the music itself.
