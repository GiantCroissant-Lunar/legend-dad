# Starlight Academy

## Overview

An ancient academy perched on a cliff overlooking the Moonlit Sea, founded by the Starweavers — scholars who could read meaning in the patterns of starlight. The Academy is a sprawling complex of white stone towers, covered walkways, and underground archives. It serves as both a center of learning and a repository for dangerous knowledge. The west wing has been sealed for thirty years since Elder Aldric's failed ritual. The Academy appears in the father's era as a place Kaelen visits for information and in the son's era as a diminished institution struggling to remain relevant.

## Atmosphere & Appearance

The Academy is built from pale limestone that glows faintly blue under starlight — a natural property of the cliff stone, not magic. During the day, it looks like weathered white marble. At night, the entire complex takes on a soft luminescence. The architecture is vertical: narrow towers with spiral staircases, observation platforms at every level, and glass-domed reading rooms. The interior is filled with star charts, orreries, and astronomical instruments. The Sealed West Wing is visible from the courtyard — its windows are dark, its doors are chained, and ivy has claimed its lower walls.

## History

Founded five hundred years ago by the Starweavers, a group of scholars who discovered that certain starlight patterns corresponded to events in the physical world. They built the Academy to study these patterns and developed a system of celestial cartography. Over centuries, the Academy's influence waned as the Starweaver bloodline thinned. Aldric's failed ritual thirty years ago was an attempt to access the Sealed Observatory at the top of the west wing, believed to contain the founders' master charts. The ritual's failure killed three scholars and collapsed the connecting bridge, effectively sealing the entire wing.

## Notable Features

- The Great Library — the largest collection of star charts in the known world, spanning three underground levels. Accessible in both eras.
- The Observatory (East) — a functioning observation dome where students study celestial patterns. Aldric teaches here.
- The Sealed West Wing — chained doors, dark windows, ivy-covered walls. Contains the Sealed Observatory at its peak. In son's era: the chains have rusted, and faint light occasionally flickers in the upper windows.
- The Courtyard of Charts — an open-air space with constellations inlaid in the stone floor. Used for outdoor lectures and ceremonies.
- The Archive Vaults — deep underground chambers containing texts deemed too dangerous for the main library. Only Aldric has the key.

## Creative Prompts

### environment-art

16-bit pixel art tileset, top-down perspective. Exterior: pale white-blue stone tiles, narrow tower structures with pointed roofs, covered walkway tiles connecting buildings, cliff-edge tiles with sea visible below. Interior library: dark wood bookshelves, reading desks with candles, star-chart scrolls on walls, orrery props (brass spheres on mechanical arms). Interior sealed wing: darker palette — dusty floors, cobwebbed furniture, cracked walls, chains across doorways. The courtyard uses special floor tiles with constellation patterns inlaid in gold against white stone. Night variant: all exterior tiles gain a soft blue luminescence, windows glow warm yellow. The contrast between the lived-in east wing (warm, lit, populated) and the sealed west wing (cold, dark, abandoned) should be stark even in top-down view.

### ambience

Exterior: sea wind, distant waves crashing on the cliff base, seabirds, the mechanical clicking of an orrery from an open window, faint sound of turning pages. Interior library: hushed — soft footsteps on stone, pages turning, quill on parchment, occasional whispered conversation, the low hum of astronomical instruments. Sealed wing: silence, then: wind through broken glass, stone settling, a faint resonance from above (the Sealed Observatory), rats. The sealed wing should feel like held breath — something waiting.

### music

Ethereal strings and soft choir in D major, slow tempo. A solo violin plays the main theme — elegant, ascending, suggesting aspiration and accumulated knowledge. Glass harmonica provides the characteristic Academy shimmer. The arrangement thins to solo piano when near the sealed wing, transposing to D minor. The choir disappears, the strings become a single sustained drone. Near the Sealed Observatory: the music drops out entirely, replaced by a single, pure tone — the resonance of whatever is sealed inside.
