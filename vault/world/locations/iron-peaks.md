# Iron Peaks

## Overview

A jagged mountain range north of Thornwall, riddled with abandoned mine tunnels and sealed caverns. The Iron Peaks are the source of the tremors threatening the region and the destination of both Kaelen's and Aric's journeys. In Kaelen's era, the peaks are dangerous but stable — the mines are abandoned, the passes are navigable, and the sealed caverns are curiosities. In Aric's era, the mountains have fractured: new crevasses split the trails, dark energy leaks from the sealed caverns, and creatures not seen in living memory emerge from the deep.

## Atmosphere & Appearance

Raw, vertical, and exposed. The lower slopes are forested with twisted iron-bark pines. Above the treeline, bare rock faces are streaked with rust-colored mineral deposits that give the peaks their name. The air thins quickly. In Kaelen's era, the peaks feel abandoned but not hostile — old mine entrances, rusted rail tracks, collapsed headframes. In Aric's era, the same landscape feels actively threatening — the rock has cracked open, faint purple light pulses from deep fissures, and the iron-bark pines near the fissures have petrified into stone.

## History

The Iron Peaks were mined for iron and copper for centuries by a now-scattered mining guild. The deepest tunnels were sealed two generations ago when miners broke through into a natural cavern system filled with a substance they couldn't identify — a crystalline material that hummed and produced faint light. Three miners died from exposure to the crystal's emissions. The guild sealed the breach, abandoned the mines, and the miners' families founded Thornwall. The sealed caverns have been a local legend ever since. Kaelen's father was one of the last miners to work the upper tunnels before the full abandonment.

## Notable Features

- The Sealed Caverns — the deepest point of the old mines, bricked up two generations ago. Whatever is inside is the source of the tremors. In father's era: sealed, inert. In son's era: the seals are cracking, light and sound leak through.
- The Upper Mines — abandoned mine tunnels used as the first dungeon area. Relatively safe in father's era (bats, cave-ins). In son's era: infested with crystal-mutated creatures.
- The Iron Pass — the main trail over the peaks to the northern regions. In father's era: passable with care. In son's era: partially collapsed, requiring alternate routes.
- Lookout Ridge — a flat outcrop with a view of Thornwall below. Used by both Kaelen and Aric as a milestone marker. In son's era, the view reveals how much the northern farms have deteriorated.
- The Petrified Grove — iron-bark pines near the fissures that have turned to stone. Only exists in Aric's era.

## Creative Prompts

### environment-art

16-bit pixel art tileset, top-down perspective. Exterior mountain tiles: gray-brown rock faces with rust-orange mineral streaks, twisted dark-green iron-bark pines on the lower slopes, gravel trails winding upward. Mine entrance tiles: dark wood framing, rusted iron rail tracks, collapsed timber supports. Interior mine tiles: rough stone walls, wooden support beams, puddles reflecting torchlight. Father's era palette: natural grays, browns, and greens with warm torchlight interiors. Son's era additions: purple-tinted fissure tiles that glow faintly, petrified tree tiles (gray stone replacing green canopy), cracked ground tiles with light bleeding through, crystal-growth tiles on cave walls (pale violet, geometric). The contrast between eras should be visible at a glance — natural browns vs. corrupted purples.

### ambience

Exterior: wind howling through rock channels (pitch varies with elevation), distant rock falls, gravel crunching underfoot, eagle cries. Father's era: wind dominates, natural mountain sounds. Son's era: add a low, rhythmic pulse from the sealed caverns (like a distant heartbeat), crystalline chiming from the fissures, and the absence of animal sounds at higher elevations. Interior mines: dripping water echoing, footsteps on stone and gravel, timber creaking under pressure. Son's era mines add: creature sounds (skittering, low growls), crystal resonance hum, and occasional deep rumbles that shake pebbles loose from the ceiling.

### music

Father's era: sparse percussion and low brass — taiko-style drums with a French horn playing a slow, ascending melody in B minor. Evokes scale and isolation, the smallness of a person against mountains. Son's era: same drums but with added distortion and uneven timing (suggesting instability), the horn melody replaced by a bowed bass played with heavy vibrato. A high, crystalline synth pad adds an alien quality. The music should feel like the mountain itself is an antagonist.
