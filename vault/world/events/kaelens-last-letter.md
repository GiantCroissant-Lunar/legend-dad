# Kaelen's Last Letter

## Overview

The last known communication from [[Kaelen]] is a letter written on the [[Iron Peaks]] trail, addressed to his son [[Aric]] and entrusted to a messenger heading south toward [[Thornwall]]. The letter was never delivered. The messenger died — cause unknown — on a remote stretch of road between the peaks and the village. Twenty years later, Aric discovers the letter in the messenger's weathered satchel, found half-buried in a roadside ditch by a traveling merchant who brought it to Thornwall's market. The letter's contents are fragmentary, damaged by weather and time, but what remains fundamentally changes Aric's understanding of his father's departure.

## Causes

Kaelen wrote the letter approximately two years after leaving Thornwall, when Aric would have been four years old. By this point, Kaelen had traveled north through the Whispering Woods and reached the upper trails of the Iron Peaks. His investigation of the sealed caverns had confirmed his worst suspicions: the seal was degrading, crystal energy was leaking into the surrounding rock, and the tremors he had felt in Thornwall were not isolated events but symptoms of a larger, accelerating process. The letter was written at a camp on the Iron Peaks trail, likely at or near Lookout Ridge, based on Kaelen's description of being able to see Thornwall's lights to the south.

Kaelen chose to write rather than return because he believed he was close to finding a way into the deeper cavern network — an alternate entrance below the sealed breach that miners' maps hinted at but never confirmed. He did not want to return empty-handed. The letter was intended as reassurance and explanation: he was alive, he was making progress, and he would come home when the work was done.

The messenger's death remains unexplained. The satchel showed no signs of violence — no blood, no weapon marks. The merchant who found it reported that the remains were skeletal, suggesting the messenger had been dead for many years, and that the satchel had been partially protected from the elements by an overhang of rock.

## Consequences

For Aric, the letter is a seismic event. He has spent twenty years constructing a narrative around his father's absence — one in which Kaelen left because he was restless, irresponsible, or simply didn't care enough to stay. The letter demolishes this narrative. It reveals a Kaelen who was afraid, who missed his son, and who believed he was sacrificing his own happiness to protect Thornwall from a genuine threat. The letter's damaged sections leave critical gaps: Kaelen describes something he found in the caverns but the passage is illegible. He mentions seeking help from someone whose name is water-damaged beyond recognition. He promises to return by a date that cannot be read.

The letter forces Aric to reconsider everything he thought he knew about his father. It does not make him forgive Kaelen — the anger of twenty years does not dissolve in a single reading — but it transforms the anger from resentment into something more complicated: grief for a relationship that might have existed, and urgency to understand what Kaelen found in the mountains that was important enough to die for. Because the letter's existence, combined with the messenger's death and Kaelen's silence in the years since, leads Aric to a conclusion he has avoided until now: his father is almost certainly dead.

The letter also provides practical information. Kaelen's descriptions of the Iron Peaks trail, the location of the alternate cavern entrance, and his observations about the crystal energy's behavior serve as a partial map for Aric's own journey north. The father's unfinished work becomes the son's starting point.
