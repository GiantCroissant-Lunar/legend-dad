# Starlight Scholars

## Overview

The scholarly order that operates the Academy of Starlight. Once a powerful institution that advised kings and predicted harvests, the Starlight Scholars have dwindled to a handful of aging academics and a few young students. They are knowledge-keepers, not warriors — their power lies in information, celestial cartography, and access to the Academy's vast archives. They are politically neutral but practically isolated, clinging to relevance in a world that has largely moved on from star-reading.

## Purpose & Goals

The Scholars' stated mission is to preserve and expand the celestial knowledge left by the Starweaver founders. In practice, they are divided: Aldric's faction prioritizes safety and controlled access to knowledge, while a smaller group of younger scholars (including Sera) believes the Academy must actively use its knowledge to address the growing crisis — the tremors, the dying forests, the sealed caverns. The Scholars do not seek political power, but their archives contain information that every other faction wants.

## Hierarchy & Structure

- **Elder** — Aldric holds this title reluctantly. The Elder has sole access to the Archive Vaults and final say on what knowledge is shared externally.
- **Starweavers** — senior scholars who can use the Starweaver Lenses. Only Aldric truly qualifies; two others claim the title based on seniority.
- **Archivists** — scholars who maintain and catalogue the library. They handle external requests for information.
- **Students** — apprentice scholars studying under Starweavers. Sera is the most advanced.

Total membership: approximately fifteen in father's era, eight in son's era.

## Territory & Influence

The Scholars control the Academy campus and the cliff it sits on. They have no military presence and rely on their remote location and reputation for protection. Their influence is informational: traders, military leaders, and other factions occasionally send envoys to request specific knowledge (crop predictions, navigation charts, historical records). The Scholars trade information for supplies and funding. In the son's era, these requests have dried up as the Academy's reputation has faded.

## Creative Prompts

### emblem

16-bit pixel art icon (16x16 and 32x32 variants). A stylized eye with a six-pointed star as the pupil, set within a circle of tiny dots representing constellations. The eye is open and upward-facing, suggesting observation of the sky. Color palette: pale blue star on white eye, silver-gray constellation dots, dark navy background circle. The design is geometric and precise — scholarly, not mystical. Used on Academy banners, scholar robes (embroidered on the collar), and official documents.

### theme-music

A chamber ensemble piece in E-flat major: harpsichord, string quartet, and glass harmonica. Formal and structured, with a waltz-like rhythm that suggests tradition and ceremony. The harpsichord carries the melody — intricate, slightly ornate, the sound of accumulated knowledge. The glass harmonica enters only in transitions, providing the signature Academy shimmer. The piece feels old — deliberately archaic in its arrangement, like music preserved in amber.

### color-palette

Primary: pale limestone white (#E8E4D8) — the Academy's stone. Secondary: celestial blue (#6B8CAE) — starlight, the lens crystal. Accent: brass gold (#B8963E) — instruments, lens frames, embroidery. Shadow: deep navy (#1A2440) — night sky, Archive Vault darkness. Highlight: silver-white (#D4DCE8) — active starlight, lens glow. The palette is cool and restrained — no warm reds or oranges. Everything suggests moonlight, old stone, and polished metal.
