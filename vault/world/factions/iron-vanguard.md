# Iron Vanguard

## Overview

A regional military force that patrols the territories between the coastal cities and the Iron Peaks. The Iron Vanguard was established a century ago to protect trade routes from bandits and mountain creatures. In Kaelen's era, they are a professional but overstretched garrison — competent soldiers led by pragmatic officers. In Aric's era, twenty years of escalating threats have transformed them into a hardened, suspicious force that controls movement through the region and views independent adventurers as liabilities rather than assets.

## Purpose & Goals

Father's era: maintain trade route safety, patrol the Iron Peaks foothills, and respond to threats reported by settlements like Thornwall. They are reactive — they deal with problems as they appear. Kaelen has a complicated relationship with the Vanguard: they respect his results but resent his independence. Son's era: the Vanguard has expanded its mandate. Faced with creatures emerging from the mountains and the collapse of the northern farmlands, they have established checkpoints, restricted travel, and begun forcibly relocating smaller settlements. Their goal has shifted from protection to control. They view the tremors as a military problem requiring military solutions and are deeply skeptical of anyone claiming it requires scholarship (the Academy) or exploration (Aric).

## Hierarchy & Structure

- **Commander** — a single regional commander based in a fortress south of the Iron Peaks. Father's era: Commander Reva, a veteran who respects results over protocol. Son's era: Commander Dain, her successor, who favors order over flexibility.
- **Captains** — field officers commanding garrisons of 20-50 soldiers. Each controls a section of road or a checkpoint.
- **Soldiers** — rank-and-file troops, mostly conscripts from coastal cities who serve three-year terms.
- **Scouts** — light infantry who patrol the mountain foothills. The most competent branch, often recruited from local populations.

Total strength: approximately 300 in father's era, 500 in son's era (expanded through conscription).

## Territory & Influence

The Vanguard controls the main road network between the coast and the mountains. Their fortress sits at the crossroads south of the Iron Peaks. In father's era, their control is light — patrols, toll stations, and a few small garrisons. In son's era, they have established hard checkpoints that require travel permits, fortified the crossroads into a walled compound, and begun patrolling deep into the Whispering Woods (much to Maren's fury). They do not control Thornwall directly, but their nearest checkpoint is close enough that Thornwall's trade depends on Vanguard goodwill.

## Creative Prompts

### emblem

16-bit pixel art icon (16x16 and 32x32 variants). A stylized iron mountain peak with a horizontal sword crossing it at the midpoint. The mountain is angular and sharp — three pointed peaks. The sword is straight and functional, not ornate. Color palette: iron gray mountain on a dark red field, steel-colored sword. The design communicates strength and rigidity. Used on soldier shields, checkpoint flags, travel permits, and branded onto Vanguard-issued equipment.

### theme-music

Military march in C minor — snare drum and low brass (trombone, tuba). The rhythm is strict and regular, emphasizing discipline and uniformity. A trumpet plays a short, declarative melody — four bars, repeated. No ornamentation, no improvisation. Father's era variant: the march is played at moderate tempo with a workmanlike quality — soldiers on patrol, doing a job. Son's era variant: the same march at a slightly faster tempo with heavier percussion, an added bass drum, and the trumpet melody played by multiple horns in unison — louder, more authoritarian, the sound of an organization that has grown beyond its original purpose.

### color-palette

Primary: iron gray (#6B6E70) — armor, weapons, practical equipment. Secondary: deep crimson (#8B2E2E) — banners, officer cloaks, authority markings. Accent: dark steel (#3D3F42) — shadows, fortification walls. Highlight: forge orange (#C47A30) — torchlight, campfires, the warm color of a working garrison. In son's era, the orange shifts cooler and the crimson deepens, suggesting the Vanguard has become less warm and more imposing. The palette is heavy and grounded — no pastels, no bright colors, everything suggests metal, stone, and fire.
