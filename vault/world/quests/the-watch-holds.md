# The Watch Holds

## Overview

A main-arc-tie-in quest in Kaelen's era, set at Lastwatch keep. After "The Vanguard's Debt" and before he reaches the Iron Peaks' sealed caverns, Kaelen is redirected by Rhen Halloway's written recommendation to pass through Lastwatch for intelligence — the keep has the best elevated view of the peaks and its warden, Jessa Vale, keeps the most consistent rockfall logs in the region. Kaelen arrives in the late afternoon to find that Jessa's watchers have already detected a small fresh fissure on the northeast face — a crack in the rock that was not there forty-eight hours ago and that is now leaking a faint violet light at dusk. The keep's standing orders require a joint-militia investigation, but the garrison is four soldiers and a warden, and two of the soldiers are down the mountain with a patrol. Jessa asks Kaelen, as a favor and with full understanding that he is not under her command, to help her and Keep Armorer Holt investigate the fissure tonight before the light pattern can be signaled to the coastal militias. This is the first time in the main arc that Kaelen sees the crystal corruption directly.

## Narrative Arc

**Act 1 — The Keep at Dusk.** Kaelen arrives at Lastwatch in the late afternoon with Rhen's written recommendation in hand. Jessa receives him at the tower door, reads the recommendation, and offers him tea at her desk. She briefs him, briefly and without drama, on what her watchers have seen: a new fissure on the northeast face, producing a faint violet glow that appears only at dusk and fades by full dark. She has logged it twice. Tonight will be the third. She is prepared to signal the observation to the coastal militias and the Starlight Academy tomorrow morning, but she would like one more direct confirmation before the signal goes out — the keep's reputation, and her own, depend on precision. Holt is already preparing the field kit. Kaelen is invited to join them. He accepts.

**Act 2 — The Climb.** Kaelen, Jessa, and Holt descend from the keep at dusk along a rope-secured path down the northeast slope to the fissure site, about half a mile from the keep and four hundred feet below its elevation. The path is maintained but steep. They reach the fissure at full dusk. It is a crack in the rock roughly three feet across and eight feet tall, emitting a steady soft violet glow at about waist height. The air near the crack has a specific smell — metallic, cold, and faintly sweet, which Kaelen recognizes from a thin account in Aldric's notes. Jessa takes the formal measurements (width, height, depth as far as a pole will reach, light-intensity by her calibrated lantern-shutter) while Holt stakes a small stone-and-iron marker at the site for tomorrow's follow-up. Kaelen holds a lantern and watches. At the deepest point of her measurement, Jessa's extended pole catches on something that is not rock, and she pulls it back with a thin layer of pale violet residue on the tip. She bags the residue carefully. The glow intensifies briefly — then stabilizes. Holt suggests they withdraw.

**Act 3 — The Signal Decision.** Back at the keep with the residue sample secured, Jessa sits at her tower desk to compose tomorrow's signal. Kaelen is present as an observer; Holt joins briefly to confirm the residue has been stored safely. The signal Jessa drafts is short — a coded flash sequence aimed at Ashenford and the Academy that will report a new fissure, its coordinates, and the presence of an unknown luminescent material. Before she sends it, she asks Kaelen's opinion on one specific detail: whether to include the residue description. Including it means the three factions will almost certainly dispatch researchers and, depending on their response, send soldiers to seal the area. Not including it means the factions will treat this as an ordinary fissure report and respond slowly if at all. The decision is Jessa's by protocol; she is consulting Kaelen as a courtesy and because he saw the residue himself. What Kaelen advises — and how he argues for it — shapes Jessa's signal, the factions' response, and the timeline by which the corruption becomes a matter of joint concern rather than a local curiosity.

## Objectives & Stakes

- **Primary objective:** Assist Warden Jessa Vale and Armorer Holt in the field measurement of the new fissure on the northeast face.
- **Secondary objective:** Observe the violet glow's behavior through the full dusk-to-dark cycle and confirm Jessa's earlier logs.
- **Optional objective:** Offer Iron Dawn's edge for Holt to examine after the climb — Holt has never seen a deep-vein-ore sword and will note in his own records that Kaelen's blade shows an unusual resonance when held near the residue sample (foreshadowing).
- **Stakes:** Immediately, a fissure on the keep's observed face. Institutionally, the timing of when the three factions become aware of the violet glow and coordinate a response. Personally, Kaelen's assessment of what he has seen — brought forward into Aldric's hands in Thornwall — becomes the most important piece of direct observation anyone outside the mining guild has about the substance underneath the Iron Peaks.

## Branching Points

1. **The Approach to Jessa.** Kaelen can present Rhen's recommendation with military formality (Jessa receives him by protocol, the interaction is clean and slightly cold), with soldier-to-soldier informality (the interaction warms — they have mutual acquaintances), or ask for Jessa's tea before business (the most generous choice; Jessa remembers this thirty years later when Aric arrives and does the same). Each choice is small. Each is remembered.

2. **Who Holds the Lantern.** On the climb in Act 2, Kaelen is assigned the lantern position (the noncombatant duty — he holds light for Jessa's measurements and Holt's marker work). He can perform the duty attentively (the normal execution), volunteer to take the pole from Jessa at the deepest measurement (offering to share the risk; Jessa declines politely — it is her measurement to take), or volunteer to set the marker for Holt (an older militia courtesy; Holt accepts, and the two men split the staking work in a way that lets Kaelen feel the vibration of the residue through the iron marker pin). Each choice gives Kaelen a slightly different piece of tactile information about the fissure.

3. **The Residue Description.** In Act 3, Jessa asks whether to include the residue in the signal. Kaelen can advocate for including it fully (maximum transparency, fastest faction response, potentially harmful to the local ecology if a seal-team is dispatched too aggressively), advocate for a hedged version (include the glow, note "unknown material, sample retained" without describing it — Jessa's preference if Kaelen says nothing), or advocate for omitting it entirely (slower response, preserves Jessa's autonomy, but costs months when the corruption accelerates). The decision Kaelen supports is the decision that gets made — Jessa trusts his field observation and will follow his argument if he makes one.

4. **Holt's Examination of Iron Dawn.** If Kaelen offers Iron Dawn for Holt to examine after the climb, the armorer notices — and notes — that the sword's edge produces a faint tone when held near the residue sample. This is the first observation of deep-vein ore responding to corruption residue, and it becomes a crucial clue Aldric draws on twenty years later when Aric encounters the same phenomenon. If Kaelen does not offer the sword, the observation is delayed by two decades and must be made independently by Aric in a later quest.

## Creative Prompts

### scene-art

16-bit pixel art, three distinct establishing shots for the three acts. Act 1 (the tower desk): Jessa Vale's quarters — a narrow room with a single east-facing window, a writing desk with three stacks of logs, a cold hearth (stated; Jessa does not light it), a narrow bed with a wool blanket. Jessa and Kaelen at the desk; Jessa has read Rhen's letter and is pouring tea from a small iron kettle. Cool silver-blue light from the window. Act 2 (the fissure): a rope-secured slope on the northeast face of the Iron Peaks, four hundred feet below Lastwatch. The keep's silhouette is visible at the top of the frame as a small black shape against a purple dusk sky. The fissure itself is foreground left — a crack in the gray rock glowing a steady soft violet at waist height, with thin threads of light visible inside like distant lightning in a cloud. Jessa in her warden's coat is taking measurements with a thin iron pole. Holt is staking a marker. Kaelen holds a lantern that is redundant next to the glow. Act 3 (the signal draft): the warden's desk again, now at full night. The only light is a single candle and a reflected glow from the signal platform above (the pre-signal brazier is already being banked for tomorrow's flash). Jessa is writing with a goose-quill; Kaelen sits across from her with his hands on the desk. The residue sample sits in a small glass vial between them, capped. The vial glows faintly, even in the candlelight.

### music

A piece in two linked movements. Movement one (Acts 1-2): the Lastwatch theme — solo French horn in C minor over a low-string drone — plays from the arrival scene through the climb, with the horn receding to a whisper during Jessa's measurements so that the ambient soundscape (wind, lantern creak, pole on rock) can take the foreground. Movement two (Act 3): the horn is joined by a solo violin playing a higher line in the parallel major, representing Jessa's signal decision. When Kaelen advocates for the signal choice, the violin's line responds — full inclusion gives a brighter, more assertive melody; hedged gives a middle-register cautious line; omission gives a thinner, trailing phrase that does not resolve. The residue sample in the glass vial has its own small sound-motif throughout: a very faint crystalline ping at irregular intervals, audible only in the quieter passages.

### sound-design

Lastwatch's high-altitude wind is the dominant ambient layer for the entire quest, varying in pitch with position — sharper at the climb site, more enclosed inside the tower, dropping to silence in the warden's room when the door is closed. Jessa's tea kettle has a specific small iron-pouring sound that features prominently in Act 1. The climb in Act 2 has a ritualized soundscape — three-person rope work, Jessa's quiet count-offs at the anchor points, Holt's deliberate hammer-on-iron when he stakes the marker (three strikes, then a pause, then three more, then a pause, the armorer's working rhythm Kaelen has now heard both at the keep's forge and at this marker). The fissure's violet glow does not have a sound of its own, but the air near it produces a small high-frequency hum that is audible only when speech is absent. The residue-sample moment has a specific short audio event: Jessa's iron pole catches the material, a faint crack of contact, and a half-second later, the glow intensifies with a soft rising tone before stabilizing. Back at the tower in Act 3, the sounds of the keep are at their most intimate — Jessa's goose-quill on parchment, the candle flickering, the distant wind, and the residue vial's occasional ping. When Jessa asks Kaelen his opinion about the signal, the ambient wind drops to near-silence for the duration of the conversation, so that whatever Kaelen says can carry.
