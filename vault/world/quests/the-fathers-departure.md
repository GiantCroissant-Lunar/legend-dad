# The Father's Departure

## Overview

The first main quest of Kaelen's era. Strange tremors have been shaking the Iron Peaks for weeks, collapsing mineshafts and splitting well-stones in Thornwall. The village militia, stretched thin by raider activity on the trade roads, cannot spare a squad to investigate. They turn to Kaelen — the only fighter in Thornwall with experience beyond the village walls. The militia captain presents him with Iron Dawn, a broadsword forged from ore pulled out of the deepest Iron Peaks mine, and asks him to find the source of the tremors before the village's foundations crack. Kaelen accepts, knowing the journey will take him away from his infant son Aric and his wife Lira. He tells himself it will be a short trip. It will not be.

## Narrative Arc

**Act 1 — The Tremor.** A tremor strikes during Thornwall's harvest festival, splitting the central well and injuring two children. The village council convenes an emergency meeting. Elder Aldric warns that the tremors match patterns he read about in old star-charts — cycles of geological instability tied to deep-earth resonance. The militia captain asks Kaelen to investigate the Iron Peaks mining tunnels where the tremors seem strongest.

**Act 2 — Preparation and Farewell.** Kaelen gathers supplies, consults Aldric's fragmentary notes, and receives Iron Dawn from the militia armory. The sword is ceremonial — forged decades ago from deep-vein ore and never used in combat. Kaelen visits Lira and infant Aric one last time. Lira does not ask him to stay; she knows he would resent it. She asks him to come back. He promises he will.

**Act 3 — The Road to the Peaks.** Kaelen travels northeast through farmland and into the rocky foothills. Along the way he encounters displaced miners, cracked roads, and an Iron Vanguard patrol that warns him the lower tunnels have been sealed by military order. The Vanguard soldiers are professional but evasive — they know more than they are sharing. Kaelen reaches the Iron Peaks mine entrance to find it barricaded. The quest ends with Kaelen deciding whether to respect the barricade or find another way in.

## Objectives & Stakes

- **Primary objective:** Reach the Iron Peaks mine entrance and assess the source of the tremors.
- **Secondary objective:** Gather information from displaced miners and Vanguard patrols about what is happening underground.
- **Optional objective:** Find Aldric's old survey markers in the foothills, placed during his research expedition thirty years ago.
- **Stakes:** Thornwall's water supply depends on the aquifer beneath the Iron Peaks. If the tremors worsen, the village loses its wells. On a personal level, every day Kaelen spends on the road is a day away from Aric — and the player should feel that weight accumulating.

## Branching Points

1. **The Militia's Gift.** When presented with Iron Dawn, Kaelen can accept it gracefully (boosting militia reputation) or reluctantly (revealing self-doubt, which Aldric notices and comments on later). This choice affects dialogue in later quests but not the plot.

2. **Lira's Farewell.** The player chooses how Kaelen says goodbye — a confident promise, an honest admission of fear, or a deflection with humor. This choice is recorded and echoed back in Son-era quests when Aric learns what his father said before leaving.

3. **The Vanguard Patrol.** Kaelen can share information with the Vanguard soldiers (cooperative approach, unlocking a shortcut through a military supply camp) or keep his mission private (independent approach, preserving surprise but missing supplies). If cooperative, the Vanguard captain mentions sealed tunnels and a "resonance" the military has been monitoring.

4. **The Barricade.** At the mine entrance, Kaelen can attempt to move the barricade (strength check, loud, alerts anything inside), search for a side passage (exploration, slower but stealthy), or turn back to report to Thornwall (delays the quest, but unlocks a preparation phase with better gear). This decision carries into the next quest.

## Creative Prompts

### scene-art

16-bit pixel art, top-down perspective with a slight 3/4 angle. Thornwall village square at dusk during the harvest festival — wooden stalls draped with orange and gold banners, villagers gathered around a cracked stone well with water seeping through the fracture. Lanterns strung between timber-frame buildings cast warm amber pools of light on cobblestones. In the foreground, Kaelen stands with Iron Dawn strapped across his back, facing the northeast road that leads into dark forested hills. A column of dust rises from the distant Iron Peaks silhouetted against a bruised purple-and-red sunset sky. The mood is bittersweet departure.

### music

Slow orchestral piece in D minor, led by a solo cello playing a descending melodic line that evokes reluctant forward motion. Strings swell gently underneath, building warmth without urgency. A single hand drum provides a quiet, steady heartbeat rhythm. At the midpoint, a solo flute enters with a counter-melody in the relative major, suggesting hope and duty intertwined. The piece ends on an unresolved suspended chord — the journey has begun but the destination is unknown. Tempo is walking pace, approximately 72 BPM.

### sound-design

Ambient layer of a village at dusk — distant conversations, the clinking of festival mugs, a dog barking twice, children laughing then going quiet as the tremor hits. The tremor itself is a deep, subsonic rumble that builds over four seconds, rattling wooden shutters and causing ceramic pots to slide off tables with sharp ceramic-on-wood scraping. Stone cracking sound: a wet, grinding snap like a tree trunk splitting but lower in pitch. After the tremor stops, a beat of total silence before the well water begins gurgling through the new fracture — a thin, persistent sound that continues under all subsequent dialogue in the scene.
