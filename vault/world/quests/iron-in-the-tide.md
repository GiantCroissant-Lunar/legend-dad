# Iron in the Tide

## Overview

A main-arc-tie-in quest in Kaelen's era, set in Saltmere Port. Three weeks into Kaelen's journey toward the Iron Peaks, he is detoured to Saltmere by a message from Elder Aldric: fishing boats have been going down in the shallows near the chalk river estuary, and the survivors are describing their hulls breaking on something invisible. Aldric believes — based on his fragmentary star-charts and a pattern he has seen only once before — that whatever is shaking the Iron Peaks is also shifting the rock beneath the coastal seabed, and that the two phenomena are the same crisis at different depths. Kaelen arrives in Saltmere to find Harbormaster Cael Vire running out of patience and options, and the port's working class running out of income. His help is requested. He does not have time to refuse.

## Narrative Arc

**Act 1 — The Harbor That Is Losing Boats.** Kaelen arrives at Saltmere Port in the late afternoon and takes a room at the Saltscale Inn. Brine serves him supper and, without being asked, tells him what everyone in the port already knows: three fishing boats in the last month, all in the same stretch of shallows near the chalk estuary, all breaking up without warning in calm weather. Two crews survived by swimming; one did not. Cael Vire is at the back table. She invites Kaelen over after he has finished his stew. She has heard from Aldric. She is not happy about it. She will take the help.

**Act 2 — What the Shallows Are Hiding.** Cael arranges a small boat and a local pilot (a fishing-boat captain named Rhodri whose cousin was on the crew that drowned) to take Kaelen out to the loss site at dawn. The chalk river estuary runs cloudy — already, even this far before the son's-era corruption — with suspended sediment that Kaelen recognizes from Aldric's samples as the same mineral residue the tremors have been scouring from beneath the Iron Peaks. Beneath the sediment, running along the seabed where the river outflow meets the tide, are ridges of freshly exposed bedrock. Old chart markings put this stretch as a clean sandy bottom thirty feet deep. The ridges now reach eighteen feet below the surface. A fishing boat hull will meet them when the tide is wrong. Kaelen dives, against Rhodri's objections, with a rope tied to the boat. The ridges are not natural — or rather, they are natural rock, but they have been pushed up from below by something that is still pushing. He feels the vibration through the stone when he puts his hand on it. It is the same vibration he felt in Thornwall's well during the tremor at the harvest festival. It is much louder here.

**Act 3 — The Warning and the Choice.** Kaelen returns to port with Rhodri, both of them shaken. Cael convenes a meeting at the Saltscale's back table: herself, Brine, Nella (who has been selling fish from the same spot for thirty years and has opinions about whose boats go where), and Rhodri. Kaelen reports what he felt. The obvious response is to close the estuary shallows to all traffic — no more lost boats. The problem is that the estuary is also Saltmere's only deep-water approach for the larger coastal traders; closing it costs the port its trade. Cael presents three options and does not choose between them. Kaelen, who is not from Saltmere and has no authority here, is asked for his view because he is the only outsider who has now touched the rock and felt it move. What he says to Cael shapes how Saltmere handles the crisis for the next twenty years — and whether the port is still standing when Aric arrives.

## Objectives & Stakes

- **Primary objective:** Investigate the cause of the fishing boat losses in the chalk estuary shallows and report to Harbormaster Cael Vire.
- **Secondary objective:** Gather local accounts from Brine (inn rumors), Nella (dock rumors), and the surviving crew members about the timing and pattern of the losses.
- **Optional objective:** Mark the ridge location on Cael's harbor chart using Kaelen's improvised sketch, so the harbor's pilots can warn larger traders away in future.
- **Stakes:** In the immediate term, more boats will be lost if the shallows remain unmarked. In the longer term, the same phenomenon Kaelen is chasing in the Iron Peaks is visibly reshaping the coastal seabed. If the port closes the estuary, Saltmere loses trade but keeps its people. If it does not, the port keeps its trade and loses more people. And underneath everything: the ridges are still rising.

## Branching Points

1. **Rhodri's Grief.** In Act 2, Kaelen can dive with Rhodri tending the rope (the efficient choice — Rhodri is a skilled sailor), or ask Rhodri to stay topside and send a different crew-hand down as backup (protecting Rhodri from reliving the loss). The second choice costs time and annoys Cael but deepens Rhodri's gratitude, which pays off in a Son-era detail when Aric meets Rhodri's adult son in Saltmere and hears a story about Kaelen's visit.

2. **The Dive.** Kaelen can examine the ridges quickly (one rope-length, one minute — enough to confirm the tremor connection), or he can push deeper (two rope-lengths, longer breath-hold — enough to find the seam where the rock is actively being pushed up, which gives Aldric's later notes much more precise data). Pushing deeper costs a saving-throw-style breath check and, if failed, Rhodri has to haul Kaelen up unconscious — embarrassing but not fatal.

3. **The Meeting's Recommendation.** At the back-table meeting, Kaelen can recommend closing the estuary (safety-first, costs Saltmere its trade), keeping it open with pilot warnings (trade-first, more lives at risk), or a middle path that closes the worst stretch seasonally and keeps a narrow marked channel open (the compromise Cael is hoping for but will not suggest herself). This choice is recorded in Cael's harbor log and echoes twenty years later when Aric reads the log in her tower.

4. **Nella's Question.** At the end of the meeting, Nella asks Kaelen, bluntly, whether he thinks the mountain and the sea are the same problem. Kaelen can answer honestly (yes, and he is on his way to the mountain), deflect (not his business, he is only here for the shallows), or lie (he does not know — hoping to spare the port the larger fear). Each answer shapes how Nella tells this story at her stall for the next twenty years, and which version Aric hears in the son's era.

## Creative Prompts

### scene-art

16-bit pixel art, top-down with a slight 3/4 tilt for the port establishing shot. Saltmere harbor at dawn — the chalk river estuary on the southeast edge of the harbor, its water a muddier teal than the clearer open sea beyond. A small fishing boat (single mast, two figures — Kaelen and Rhodri — in the stern) sits at anchor about fifty yards offshore, with a rope descending from the gunwale into the water. Below the surface, visible as a paler-blue underlayer of the water tile, a ridge of rock shows in a rough line running with the river's outflow — about four feet below the hull. The sky is low silver-gray cloud with a band of rose at the eastern horizon. Gulls circle at a distance. On the foreground dock, Cael Vire stands watching with her hands in her coat pockets, Brine beside her with a mug of hot broth for when Kaelen comes up. The mood is tense working-morning — not heroic, not dangerous, just the kind of work that may go wrong at any moment without warning.

### music

Slow orchestral piece in C minor for bowed bass, low strings, and a solo oboe. The oboe carries the melody over a bass drone that suggests underwater pressure. At the moment of the dive, the surface instruments drop out and a single muted cello plays a downward line with small quiet silences between notes — the sound of breath-holding and depth. A low, very quiet, non-instrumental hum sits underneath from the moment Kaelen's hand touches the ridge: the same resonance frequency Thornwall's harvest-festival tremor introduced in "The Father's Departure," now at a lower pitch, felt in the coastal bass rather than the mountain strings. When the oboe returns at the port meeting, it is slightly sharp. The tuning has changed. Tempo is slow, approximately 58 bpm.

### sound-design

Coastal working-port ambience as the constant bed — gulls, slow-slapping water against pilings, rope creaking, distant ship bells — layered over the whole quest. For the dive scene, an underwater wash filter applied to everything topside, plus a clear foreground sound of Kaelen's own slow exhale through pursed lips (no breathing apparatus — just a held breath with a controlled release). The moment his hand touches the ridge: a low sub-audible rumble of about 40 Hz, rising to 50 Hz over three seconds and then plateauing. Small granular stone-cracking sounds fade in and out as the resonance builds. When he breaks the surface after the dive, the underwater wash cuts abruptly and Rhodri's voice is the first sound — not a shout, a question, quiet and worried. The back-table meeting is a deliberately small soundscape: the Saltscale Inn's cooking fire, a single lamp sputtering, mugs on wood, no music, and the outside of the inn (harbor sounds) filtered through a closed door. When Nella asks her final question, the room goes completely silent for one beat before Kaelen answers.
