# The Vanguard's Debt

## Overview

A main-arc-tie-in quest in Kaelen's era, set in Ashenford. After "The Father's Departure," Kaelen reaches Ashenford and requests passage through the canyon to the Iron Peaks. In the cooperative era of the Iron Vanguard, passage is not refused outright — but it is not free, either. Captain Rhen Halloway extends the courtesy of the garrison's supply depot (food, cartography, a rest) in exchange for Kaelen's help with a small matter: a Vanguard supply caravan has been held up by a rockslide in the southern pass, and the escort squad that was sent to clear it has not yet reported back. Rhen needs a second party to ride out, confirm the squad is alive, and — if the road is genuinely blocked — help reorganize the caravan onto an alternate route. It is a day's work. It is also a test, though Rhen does not call it one. How Kaelen handles this favor is what earns him the kind of cooperation that will matter when he reaches the canyon mouth two days later.

## Narrative Arc

**Act 1 — The Request.** Kaelen arrives at Ashenford in the early afternoon. He is not expected but he is not obstructed; the southern gate sentries direct him to the Traveler's Hearth (the civilian inn) for the night and note his name in the passage log. Before he can unpack, Quartermaster Berin finds him in the common room and delivers a brief, formal message: Captain Halloway requests a meeting at his convenience. Kaelen goes. Rhen receives him in the Command Hall, offers him tea, and asks — not orders, asks — whether Kaelen would be willing to assist with a small patrol matter that has come up. Rhen describes the caravan, the rockslide, and the escort squad's silence. He does not dress the request up as a quid pro quo, but he is clear that Kaelen's cooperation will shape the kind of help the Vanguard can offer him on the road north.

**Act 2 — The Southern Pass.** Kaelen rides out with a guide — a young Vanguard corporal named Sier — and reaches the rockslide site by midafternoon. The pass is blocked, genuinely, by a fresh fall of gray-brown rock that does not look like weathering; it looks like the ground shifted. The escort squad is alive and working to clear the rubble by hand, but one of them (a sergeant named Terrell) is injured — a leg broken when the slide started. The caravan is intact, parked two hundred yards up the road, with its three teamsters and eight mules. The squad has been working for a day and a half without word to Ashenford because their signal-rider was under rubble for the first four hours (he is alive; Terrell pulled him out) and the signal-horn itself is cracked. Kaelen's arrival is their first contact with the outside since the fall.

**Act 3 — The Choice of Routes.** The rockslide will take another two days to clear by hand and possibly longer if the ground shifts again. The alternate route — a longer, older road that runs east around a small set of ridges — is passable but unused for years and will take the caravan an extra four days to cover. Kaelen must help Rhen's squad decide: dig out the main road (risky; the ground is visibly unstable, and the squad is already short on equipment) or reroute the caravan east (safer, but costly in time and exposing the caravan to an area the Vanguard has not patrolled in a year). Kaelen's knowledge of Thornwall's well-stone fractures — the same shifting-ground pattern he saw at the harvest festival — lets him recognize that the slide site is actively unsafe. What he argues for, how he argues, and whether he offers to ride ahead on the eastern route to scout are all choices that get reported back to Rhen — and that shape Rhen's view of him.

## Objectives & Stakes

- **Primary objective:** Locate the missing escort squad and report their status back to Captain Rhen Halloway.
- **Secondary objective:** Help the squad make a viable decision between clearing the main road and rerouting the caravan east.
- **Optional objective:** Assess Sergeant Terrell's broken leg and improvise a splint from travel-pack materials (Kaelen's Thornwall militia training covers basic field medicine; a clean splint wins Terrell's gratitude and a later-quest favor).
- **Stakes:** The immediate stakes are the caravan's safety and the squad's extraction. The larger stakes, for Kaelen, are the relationship with the Vanguard: if he handles this favor well, Rhen extends him a deeper courtesy at Ashenford that makes his onward journey markedly easier. If he handles it poorly — or refuses — the cooperation ends at the civilian inn and nothing more.

## Branching Points

1. **Whether to Ride Out Immediately.** Kaelen can accept Rhen's request and leave within the hour (the Vanguard's preference, and what Sier expects) or ask for a night's rest first (reasonable, he just walked a long way — but costs him a half-day and means the caravan waits longer). The delayed version produces a small cost-benefit scene: Rhen does not push, Sier is visibly disappointed, and one mule in the caravan goes lame overnight, which complicates Act 3.

2. **Terrell's Splint.** In Act 2, Kaelen can splint Terrell's leg himself (drawing on his Thornwall militia first-aid — reliable, direct), delegate to Sier under his instruction (teaches the corporal, slower but a small long-term investment in Sier's competence), or defer to the squad's own field medic (respectful of chain of command, but the medic is exhausted and makes a mistake). Each choice is observed by the squad and reported.

3. **The Route Argument.** In Act 3, Kaelen can argue strongly for rerouting east (safest, costs time), argue for digging the main road (fastest, risks another slide), or present both options and defer to Terrell as the senior soldier on site (respectful, but means the squad's exhausted sergeant has to make a call he is not in shape for). Rhen's follow-up at Ashenford depends on which path was chosen and how the choice was made — a cooperative advocate who supported the sergeant's judgment is the version Rhen most respects.

4. **Sier's Future.** If Kaelen has made a good impression on the young corporal (by teaching him the splint, by listening to him on the ride, by including him in the Act 3 argument), Sier writes a private letter to Rhen after the return that becomes a quiet paragraph in Kaelen's personnel note — the paragraph that twenty years later lets Rhen, in retirement, recognize Aric as Kaelen's son from across the refugee camp without needing to be told.

## Creative Prompts

### scene-art

16-bit pixel art, wide establishing shot for the Act 2 rockslide. A rocky southern pass between two gray-brown ridges, the road cut through it as a narrow flagstone strip now buried for about forty feet by a spill of fresh gray-brown rubble. The rubble is pale and dusty, visibly recent — no moss, no weathering, no darker weathered stone mixed in. Two Vanguard soldiers work at the rubble with pickaxes; a third sits on a flat rock with his leg splinted in rope and cut branches, watching the road behind him. A cart — the injured signal-rider's rig — stands at the edge of the slide with its mule hobbled. Kaelen and Sier approach on foot (they tied their horses at the safe end of the pass). The sky is overcast, flat gray, with the wind-direction cues visible in a thin banner on a trailing rope. In the far background, the peaks of the Iron range are visible as silhouettes — the destination waiting. The mood is working problem-solving, not heroic.

### music

A subdued march in F minor for snare drum and low brass, matched tempo-wise to the steady rhythm of picks on stone. The snare cadence is crisp but unhurried; the brass melody is simple and repeating, the sound of an institution doing its everyday work. At the moment Kaelen arrives at the slide site, a solo fiddle enters at half the march's volume with Kaelen's own theme (his solo cello/guitar motif from "The Father's Departure"), and the two musics — Vanguard march and Kaelen's cello-guitar — play in counterpoint, neither dominating. The piece resolves into a shared key only at the end, when the route decision is made — suggesting cooperation earned rather than assumed. Tempo is 80 bpm. Small changes in the snare pattern mark the different branching outcomes of Act 3.

### sound-design

Mountain-pass ambience — wind funneled between ridges (pitched high, fluctuating), gravel shifting underfoot on dry rock, distant crows, an occasional small rockfall in the distance that makes everyone at the slide site stop and listen for a half-second before resuming work. The sound of the squad at work is specific and matters: three different pick-on-stone rhythms (each soldier working at their own pace), a grunt, the scrape of a shovel, the creak of a rope strained taut against a large stone being levered aside. Terrell's breathing when Kaelen examines his leg is shallow and controlled — a sergeant keeping his composure in front of his men; when Kaelen asks quietly if it hurts, Terrell's exhale is louder and more honest. The splint-making has its own small soundscape: cloth tearing, branches being trimmed with a knife, rope looping tight. At the Act 3 conversation, the squad gathers in a rough half-circle around a flat rock that serves as the map table; the acoustics compress into a small enclosed soundfield, and the wind drops below their voices. When a decision is made, the wind picks back up.
