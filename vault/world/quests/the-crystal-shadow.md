# The Crystal Shadow

## Overview

A side quest in Aric's era, set at Lastwatch keep. On his way from Ashenford toward the sealed caverns, Aric is advised by a refugee at Hollow's Rest (an old joint-militia veteran who served at Lastwatch in his youth) to stop at the keep — not for supplies, because there are none, and not for intelligence from the current factions, because there isn't any, but because Warden Jessa Vale has been alone at her post for thirty years, and if Aric is going into the Peaks, she is the one person who will know what he is walking into. Aric and Maren arrive at the partially crystal-consumed keep in late afternoon, find the warden alive and keeping the signal fire lit, and learn from her — in the course of a single evening — a piece of what his father saw when he passed through thirty years ago. At dusk, Aric sees the Crystal-Shunted Watchman on the signal platform for a single minute. Whether that encounter means anything or is simply what Lastwatch now produces is left deliberately ambiguous.

## Narrative Arc

**Act 1 — The Half-Claimed Keep.** Aric and Maren approach Lastwatch along the high trail in the late afternoon. The keep's silhouette is unmistakable against the gray sky — a central tower, a broken outer wall on the north face, the signal platform's brazier visible at the top. As they get closer, the extent of the crystal-growth becomes visible: pale violet clusters climbing the bailey walls, the stables completely taken, a lattice pattern across the armory's sealed door. The only part of the keep that is clear is the central tower, from the ground floor up, and the signal platform above it. Jessa Vale meets them at the tower door. She is sixty-two and has lived here alone for thirty years. She reads them both at a glance — Aric's line in particular — and invites them inside for tea. She does not comment on the keep's condition. She does not apologize for it.

**Act 2 — Tea at the Warden's Desk.** Inside the warden's tower room, at the same desk where she wrote her signal thirty years ago, Jessa serves tea from the same small iron kettle. She asks Aric direct, brief questions — name, route, how he came to be on this trail — and answers his in the same manner. Over the course of an hour, Aric learns: that Jessa remembers his father passing through with Rhen Halloway's recommendation; that Kaelen helped her with a fissure-survey on the northeast face; that she has kept the residue sample from that night in a sealed iron box on the desk's bottom shelf for thirty years, and that the sample still faintly glows. She produces the box. She opens it. The vial inside has not changed. Aric looks at it. Maren looks at it. The glow is the same glow that Aric has seen in the Whispering Woods' sinkholes and on the Wounded Sentry's arm. Jessa says, simply: "I thought you would want to know it has been here." She re-seals the box.

**Act 3 — The Signal Platform at Dusk.** Before they leave, Jessa invites Aric up to the signal platform for the nightly lighting of the brazier. Maren stays below. On the platform, Jessa works the pulley mechanism that tilts the brazier-pan, and she and Aric lay the tinder and strike the flame. The fire catches; the keep lights its lone remaining signal. Aric, standing at the southwest edge of the platform, sees a figure. A young man in an old joint-militia coat stands at the far corner, looking south-southwest toward the Iron Peaks. His right shoulder is fused with pale violet crystal that extends down into the stone of the platform beneath him. He does not turn. When Aric looks at Jessa — who has seen him too, and who gives Aric a small, composed nod that means "you are seeing what I see" — and then looks back, the figure is no longer there. Jessa notes the sighting in her log (her sixty-fourth). She does not explain. Aric does not ask.

## Objectives & Stakes

- **Primary objective:** Meet Warden Jessa Vale, take tea with her, and collect whatever intelligence she has about the sealed caverns and what lies ahead.
- **Secondary objective:** View the residue sample Kaelen and Jessa collected thirty years ago, and confirm it matches what Aric has seen in the Whispering Woods.
- **Optional objective:** Attend the nightly lighting of the signal brazier with Jessa on the platform at dusk — the invitation is not compulsory, and refusing it keeps Aric away from the Crystal-Shunted Watchman entirely.
- **Stakes:** The intelligence Jessa carries is concrete and specific — a firsthand account of the first fissure on the northeast face, a residue sample thirty years old, and a record of the keep's deterioration since — all of which directly informs Aric's approach to the sealed caverns. The sighting on the platform is ambiguous by design: the Watchman is not explained, and the quest does not make Aric decide what he is. The stakes of that encounter are emotional rather than mechanical: Aric carries the image with him into the Iron Peaks.

## Branching Points

1. **How Aric Introduces Himself.** At the tower door, Aric can introduce himself by name only (neutral, formal), introduce himself and mention his father (directly invoking Kaelen — Jessa will confirm she remembers him without making a production of it), or let Jessa recognize him first (she does, silently, within two seconds of seeing him; waiting for her to name it creates a small specific kind of warmth). Each opening sets a slightly different tone for the tea conversation.

2. **The Residue Sample.** When Jessa produces the sealed iron box, Aric can ask to examine the vial closely (Jessa permits it; Aric handles the vial himself, feels its faint warmth, and feels through the glass the same low resonance he felt in the Whispering Woods sinkholes), ask questions but not touch it (Jessa describes what she has observed over thirty years), or thank her for showing him and ask her to re-seal it immediately (a choice that honors Jessa's stewardship and makes her smile, her first real smile of the visit). Each choice teaches Aric something different about the residue.

3. **The Invitation to the Platform.** When Jessa invites Aric up for the brazier lighting, Aric can accept (he will see the Crystal-Shunted Watchman), decline politely and stay below with Maren (he will not see the Watchman, and Jessa will light the signal alone as she has done for thirty years), or ask Maren whether she wants to go instead (Maren declines; she has decided, privately, that the platform is Jessa's place and she will not intrude). Whether Aric sees the Watchman at all is his choice to make.

4. **Whether to Ask About the Watchman.** If Aric sees the Watchman on the platform and wishes to ask Jessa about him, he has three options: ask immediately while still on the platform (Jessa answers briefly, factually, without elaboration — a specific answer: "I see him at dusk. He does not speak. I believe he is what happens when a watchman remains after everyone else has gone"), ask downstairs over a second cup of tea (Jessa answers more fully, and shows Aric her log of sightings), or do not ask at all (the encounter becomes something Aric carries privately, and Jessa respects his choice). Each answer's weight is different. None of them explain the Watchman. That is the point.

## Creative Prompts

### scene-art

16-bit pixel art, three distinct scene compositions. Act 1 (the approach): a wide establishing shot of Lastwatch from the high trail, showing the partially crystal-claimed keep in late-afternoon light. The central tower rises from the crystal-climbed bailey walls; the north wall is cracked open; the stable block is a lattice of pale violet. The signal platform atop the tower is visible with its brazier-frame. The sky is low silver-gray with a faint band of pink at the horizon. Aric and Maren are small figures on the trail, approaching the keep's tower door. Act 2 (the warden's desk): Jessa's tower room interior — the small iron kettle on the desk, two mugs of tea, the sealed iron box set out between them with its lid open and the glowing vial visible inside. Warm candlelight balances cool silver-blue from the window; the glow from the vial is a third light source, small and specific. Jessa in her patched warden's coat; Aric leaning forward across the desk; Maren standing by the wall, arms crossed, listening. Act 3 (the signal platform at dusk): a high wide shot of the platform atop the tower, the brazier just catching flame and beginning to throw warm orange light across the stone. Jessa and Aric stand near the brazier. At the far southwest edge of the platform, a figure is visible — a young man in an old joint-militia coat, facing away, his right shoulder fused with pale violet crystal that extends down into the stone of the platform beneath him. The figure is drawn slightly translucent; a viewer who looks at him directly sees him; a viewer whose eye moves away sees only stone. The dusk sky is a deep indigo-purple with the first star visible. The Iron Peaks are silhouettes to the south.

### music

A piece for violin (Jessa's theme) and cello (Kaelen's theme, played in absence). In Act 1, only the violin — Jessa's line from the Lastwatch location music — plays, thinly, over wind. In Act 2, as the iron kettle pours and the residue sample is produced, the cello enters quietly, playing Kaelen's theme from thirty years ago; the two instruments do not play in the same key, and their lines intertwine without resolving, like two memories that share a space without agreeing. In Act 3, on the platform, both instruments drop out and are replaced by the Crystal-Shunted Watchman's theme — a single pitched crystal tone, joined after eight seconds by a second tone a perfect fifth above, joined after another eight seconds by a very faint quarter-tone off from the fifth. When the Watchman is no longer there, the crystal tones fade but do not immediately stop; they are present at very low volume under the return of Jessa's violin, which plays alone while Jessa logs the sighting in her book. The piece ends when the entry is written.

### sound-design

Lastwatch's signature wind soundscape (pitched high, varying with wall geometry) is the constant ambient bed for the entire quest. The approach up the high trail has gravel-crunch footsteps, wind, and the keep's silhouette producing no additional sound until Aric is close enough to hear the crystal growths' high-frequency hum — which begins at the edge of perception and strengthens with proximity. Inside the tower, the wind is muffled; the iron kettle has a specific pouring sound; tea cups on wood; Jessa's pen logging the visit. The residue vial, when Jessa opens the iron box, does not make a sound on opening, but the vial produces a very faint crystalline ping once every few seconds, audible in the quiet of the room. On the platform at dusk, the wind rises again; the brazier catches with a specific whoomph of wood-and-oil; the Watchman's presence corresponds to a slight drop in the wind's volume (as if the platform's acoustics change when he is there) and a faint subsonic hum that is felt rather than heard. When Aric looks away and looks back and the Watchman is no longer there, the wind returns to full volume and the hum is gone. Jessa's pen on her log-book in the final moment is the quietest sound in the entire quest and is the last thing the player hears before the scene ends.
