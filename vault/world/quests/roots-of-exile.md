# Roots of Exile

## Overview

A main-arc-tie-in quest in Aric's era, introducing Hollow's Rest. After the Whispering Woods investigation, Maren tells Aric — for the first time — that she knows a settlement deep in the woods that he needs to see. She has not spoken of it to anyone, including Aric, in the year since she started visiting. She has reasons. She takes him there now because Physician Wren's supplies for Thornwall have started arriving late, the patients Wren treats are getting worse, and Wren believes Torbin is withholding information. Aric's job is to deliver Thornwall's current tithe of medicinal herbs, receive the settlement's request list for the next month, and — if Torbin is willing to speak with him — find out what the old man has been holding back. What Aric finds instead is a village of fifty people living in the hollow of a tree-fall, an elder who taught him his letters, and a piece of news that changes his understanding of why his father left.

## Narrative Arc

**Act 1 — The Path That Is Not a Path.** Maren and Aric leave the Whispering Woods' edge before dawn with a supply pack of Thornwall herbs. Maren leads by a route that is not a path — through a swampy low ground, across a fallen log bridge, over a stream at a place where the footprints do not hold. Aric does not speak during most of the walk. He is thinking about why Maren has kept this from him. Three hours in, at the edge of a thicket, Maren stops and gives him a small woven signal-token to tie to his wrist — everyone at Hollow's Rest wears one, and entering without one will get a bird-call whistled and the settlement will hide. Aric ties it on. They enter the tree-fall hollow at midmorning, and the settlement sees them before they see the settlement — children at the Listening Stones, two hunters at the north edge, the whole hollow quietly going still.

**Act 2 — The Elder at His Door.** Torbin is at the door of his cottage when Maren walks Aric into the Hearth Circle. He sees Aric before Aric sees him. He does not cry out; he does not move toward them; he simply stops what he was doing and looks. He knew Aric as a child. He taught him his letters alongside his wife Elen. He has not seen him in three years. Aric does not recognize him immediately — the old man has thinned, and the beard is new — but when Torbin says his name, quietly, the recognition happens. Aric sets down the supply pack. Maren steps back. This is not her scene. Torbin invites Aric inside. Physician Wren, who has watched the arrival from the doorway of the Midwife's Hearth, gives the two of them ten minutes before she walks over with the settlement's supply list and joins the conversation.

**Act 3 — The Sealed Tube.** Inside Torbin's cottage, on a small writing desk, is a leather tube sealed with a knot Aric recognizes from his father's journal — the same knot Kaelen used to mark things he did not want read by anyone but the intended recipient. Torbin tells Aric, directly and without rehearsal, what he has been holding back: Kaelen wrote him a letter from the Iron Peaks thirty years ago, and it was the last correspondence Kaelen sent to anyone. Aldric at Thornwall burned the letter Kaelen sent to him — Aric learned this from Maren during "Whispers in the Woods." What Aric did not know is that Kaelen sent two letters: one to Aldric, and one to Torbin. Aldric burned his. Torbin did not. The sealed tube on the writing desk contains Kaelen's letter. Torbin has not opened it. He has been waiting, he says, for Aric to be the person who opens it. Aric can open it now, refuse to open it, or ask Torbin to read it aloud while he listens.

## Objectives & Stakes

- **Primary objective:** Deliver Thornwall's current herb tithe to Physician Wren and receive the supply list for the next month.
- **Secondary objective:** Meet Torbin and establish the terms of Thornwall's continued quiet support of Hollow's Rest (Maren has been handling this alone; Aric now shares the responsibility).
- **Optional objective:** Open, or ask Torbin to open, the sealed letter Kaelen sent thirty years ago.
- **Stakes:** For Hollow's Rest, the supply relationship with Thornwall is a lifeline and needs to be formalized with a second person who knows the route and the protocols. For Aric, the letter is the first direct communication from his father that is not mediated by a legend — the first document in Kaelen's own hand that Aric can read. For Torbin, passing the letter to Aric is the release of a burden he has carried for thirty years.

## Branching Points

1. **Why Maren Did Not Tell.** In Act 1, at the fallen-log bridge, Aric can ask Maren directly why she has kept Hollow's Rest from him. She has three answers depending on how he asks: if he asks coldly, she tells him she was protecting the settlement from a Thornwall that might use the information badly; if he asks honestly, she tells him she was protecting him from the weight of knowing; if he asks with curiosity rather than accusation, she tells him the truth — both of those things, and she was not sure which he needed more until this season. Aric's tone here affects Maren's trust in the conversations of the next two acts.

2. **Recognizing Torbin.** In Act 2, Aric can recognize Torbin immediately when the old man says his name (acknowledging the past, a warm moment), fail to recognize him and have Torbin volunteer his identity (awkward but honest), or actively deny the recognition because he does not want to have this moment in public (cold, wounding, sets the tone of the interior conversation poorly). The emotional reading of the act hinges on which of these Aric does.

3. **The Letter.** In Act 3, Aric can open the sealed tube himself (direct, full weight on him), ask Torbin to read it aloud (shared weight, slower emotional landing), or refuse to open it at all (putting the tube back on the desk; Torbin accepts this without argument and the tube stays closed until Aric decides otherwise). If Aric opens the letter, its contents matter — Kaelen wrote that he had reached the sealed tunnels, that the thing inside was not what Aldric's charts predicted, and that he could not come back because coming back would bring it with him. He asked Torbin to raise his son if Lira could not. He did not know Elen would raise Aric instead. The letter ends: "If my son reads this, tell him I was not brave. I was only afraid of the right thing."

4. **Wren's Supply List.** Parallel to the letter scene, Wren's supply list is handed to Aric. He can take it at face value (standard supply-line response), ask her directly what she thinks about the crystal corruption's advance through the forest (opens up a side conversation that gives Wren and Aric a working relationship for future quests), or press her on what Torbin has been withholding (Wren tells him about the sealed tube before Torbin does — which changes Torbin's choice in Act 3, and makes Aric's position colder).

## Creative Prompts

### scene-art

16-bit pixel art, top-down with dappled-canopy overlay for the Hollow's Rest scenes. Act 1 (the path): a side-scrolling perspective of Maren ahead of Aric, walking a fallen log across a mist-wreathed low ground, the forest floor below them green-black with standing water. The woven signal-tokens on their wrists catch a small shaft of morning light — these are the brightest specks of color in the frame. Act 2 (the Hearth Circle): top-down of the tree-fall hollow, the Hearth Circle's low stone ring at center with a small fire going, Torbin standing at the door of his root-arch cottage with his left hand pressed against his knee. The settlement's other cottages ring the hollow in a soft curve. Dappled green-gold light falls through the intact canopy. Act 3 (inside Torbin's cottage): a small cramped interior — a low table with a writing desk to one side, the sealed leather tube on the desk catching the thin light from a single small window, a cold hearth, a comb on a shelf (Elen's), two chairs. Aric is seated. Torbin is on his feet. The room's palette is warm wood and candlelight; the tube is the focal point of the frame.

### music

Solo mandolin in G dorian from "Hollow's Rest" location theme carries the walk-in in Act 1, entering at the moment Aric and Maren cross the fallen log. The piece shifts tempo in Act 2 — the mandolin slows and is joined by Torbin's wooden recorder (from his character theme), the two instruments playing together for the first time. In Act 3, inside Torbin's cottage, both instruments drop out and are replaced by a single sustained note on a bowed psaltery. When Aric makes a choice about the letter, the music responds: opening the letter introduces Kaelen's cello theme quietly, playing underneath as the letter is read; asking Torbin to read it aloud keeps only the psaltery and adds Torbin's recorder as a quiet counter-melody; refusing to open it leaves only the psaltery, fading slowly, for a full twenty seconds. The music's choice is Aric's choice. There is no single correct setting.

### sound-design

The walk-in's soundscape is the Whispering Woods' healthy-pocket version — birdsong, real and present, and the sound of feet on wet forest floor that alternates between soft moss, woody log, and a brief splash at the stream crossing. The canopy overhead has a very specific whispering quality in this pocket: the old version of the forest's sound, the one that gave the woods their name, before the corruption changed the pitch. Hollow's Rest itself has Torbin's ambience — low fires, quiet voices, children hushed by parents, a broom on packed earth — and everything goes very still when Aric and Maren arrive. The stillness is the sound of a settlement assessing a visitor. Inside Torbin's cottage, the soundscape contracts to the small interior: the cold hearth makes no sound, the window lets in a thin filtered outside, and the sealed tube, when Torbin places it in Aric's hand, has the dry texture of old leather and a very small internal rustle of paper. If Aric opens it, the knot unties with a single soft pull and the parchment unfolds with three small crisp sounds. If Torbin reads it aloud, his voice is the only sound in the room for the duration. If Aric refuses, the tube is set back on the desk with a small final click and the scene holds in silence for a beat before the psaltery note fades.
