# The Old Elder's Map

## Overview

A side quest in Aric's era, set at Hollow's Rest and in the surrounding woods. After the events of "Roots of Exile," Torbin produces a second item he has been holding since Thornwall — a map that Kaelen drew in his own hand the week before he left for the Iron Peaks, annotated in Kaelen's shorthand with sketched features that do not appear on any Thornwall survey. The map is a working document — folds, tea stains, one corner burned — and Torbin has never walked most of it himself. He asks Aric and Maren to confirm the features Kaelen marked, in particular two small landmarks in the deep woods that Torbin has never been able to find. The quest is a small field-cartography run: walk the map, verify what Kaelen sketched, and note what has changed in thirty years. What Aric finds is that his father was better at this kind of work than anyone remembered — and that one of Kaelen's marked features still exists, exactly where he said it would.

## Narrative Arc

**Act 1 — The Map at the Writing Desk.** The morning after "Roots of Exile," Torbin shows Aric a folded piece of old parchment he has kept in a small cedar box on his writing desk. It is Kaelen's hand — Aric knows it from the journal entries he read as a child in the Thornwall militia barracks. The map covers a rough triangle of deep Whispering Woods between Hollow's Rest, the old hunter's road, and the foothills of the Iron Peaks. Two features are marked with small notes: a "deep spring" and a "stone circle." Torbin has searched for the deep spring once, alone, a year ago, and could not find it. He never searched for the stone circle. He asks Aric and Maren — Aric for the eyes that recognize his father's shorthand, Maren for the forester's skill — to walk the map and mark what they confirm.

**Act 2 — The Deep Spring.** Maren leads the walk. The first landmark on the map is the deep spring, a short half-day's walk southeast of Hollow's Rest. They find it roughly where Kaelen marked it, but slightly offset — not where the map says, but where the map's shorthand annotation indicates the spring seasonally moves to, which Aric has to translate for Maren. The spring itself is unchanged: a small basin of cold clear water emerging from a mossy crevice in a rock-face, ringed with ferns that are still green despite the corruption elsewhere. Aric recognizes, with a small shock, that this is a pocket of still-healthy forest like Hollow's Rest itself — a second one, small, that no one in the settlement has known about. Maren marks it in her own field-book. They refill their water skins.

**Act 3 — The Stone Circle.** The second landmark, the stone circle, is a full day's walk to the north-northeast, near where the forest begins to thin into the foothills. The map notes it as "7 stones, chest-high, ~20 ft across." Aric and Maren reach the coordinates in the late afternoon. The clearing exists but the circle does not — or rather, it does, and it does not. Six of the seven stones are gone, toppled and half-overgrown with crystal-adjacent fungal growth. The seventh stone stands. It is chest-high, roughly carved, and on its inner face, there is a small symbol Kaelen sketched on the margin of the map: a circle with three lines through it, which Aric has never seen anywhere else. Under the single standing stone, half-buried in leaf-mold, is a small wooden box. Inside the box is a letter — not to Aric, not to Torbin, not to Lira. It is addressed "To Whoever Finds This." Kaelen left it thirty years ago.

## Objectives & Stakes

- **Primary objective:** Walk Kaelen's map and verify the locations of the deep spring and the stone circle.
- **Secondary objective:** Record what has changed in thirty years at each site — in particular, which features are still there and which are gone.
- **Optional objective:** Bring back a small sample of the deep spring's still-healthy ferns for Physician Wren (she has been looking for pockets of uncorrupted flora for six months and this is the first Aric can provide).
- **Stakes:** The immediate stakes are small — a confirmation exercise for Torbin, a possible supply scout for Wren. The larger stakes are the stone circle: Kaelen left something there that is not in his journal, not in any letter, not in Aldric's records. The symbol on the standing stone is not a Thornwall symbol or a Vanguard symbol. It is something Kaelen found and recorded. Whether Aric shares it with anyone in Hollow's Rest changes what kind of investigation the settlement can undertake next.

## Branching Points

1. **The Shorthand Translation.** At the deep spring in Act 2, Aric recognizes his father's cartographic shorthand — a notation that indicates a spring with seasonal offset. Aric can translate the shorthand aloud to Maren (sharing his father's expertise, a small warm moment), work through it privately in his head (keeping it to himself, which Maren notices but does not comment on), or dismiss it as inscrutable (a subtle choice to put distance between himself and his father's competence). The translation is trivial mechanically. The choice is entirely character.

2. **The Sample for Wren.** Aric can gather a small cutting of the deep spring's ferns (carefully, not disturbing the ecosystem — Maren shows him the correct technique), refuse on the grounds that disturbing the pocket might weaken it (a legitimate ecological concern Maren shares), or take a larger sample out of curiosity (Maren disapproves; Wren receives the sample but warns Aric that she can do less with it because of how it was gathered). The ecological ethics of the son's era are an ongoing theme.

3. **The Standing Stone.** When Aric sees the symbol carved on the inside of the seventh stone, he can copy it into his own field-book carefully (preserving the record), ignore it as a curiosity (mistake — it matters later), or ask Maren if she recognizes it (she does not, but she will remember it, and this memory pays off in a much later quest at the Starlight Academy).

4. **The Letter Addressed to No One.** The wooden box contains a letter in Kaelen's hand, addressed generically. Aric can read it now at the stone (the obvious choice), take it back to Hollow's Rest unread and read it with Torbin (sharing the discovery, strengthens their bond), or take it back sealed and keep it for himself (a private version of what Torbin did for thirty years). The letter's contents are the same regardless: Kaelen writes that he has found a pattern of marked stones that predate any settlement he knows, that the symbols resemble no living language, and that he believes — "believes, without proof" — that someone walked this forest before the current age and knew the sealed caverns were coming. He signs it "K." There is no date and no return address.

## Creative Prompts

### scene-art

16-bit pixel art, three small scene compositions. Act 1 (Torbin's writing desk): the folded parchment being smoothed open on the desk surface, Aric's hands and Torbin's hands visible in frame, Kaelen's handwriting in small careful black ink across the map, tea-stain ring on one corner, burn mark on another, candlelight from a single taper. Act 2 (the deep spring): a small clearing in a pocket of still-healthy forest, a dark rock-face with a mossy crevice at its base and clear water spilling into a shallow basin ringed with green ferns. Maren kneels at the basin's edge, testing the water with her fingertips; Aric stands behind her, reading the map annotation. The canopy is intact here; the light is the dappled gold-green of the father's-era woods. Act 3 (the stone circle): a clearing in late-afternoon light where the forest is thinning into rocky foothill. Six toppled stones ring a seventh that still stands upright, roughly chest-high. Pale violet fungal growth climbs the fallen stones. The standing stone's inner face, visible to the viewer because of how Aric is positioned, shows a small carved circle with three lines through it. A small wooden box, half-buried in leaf-mold, is at the base of the standing stone. Aric is kneeling; Maren is watching the treeline.

### music

A quiet piece for solo mandolin (from Hollow's Rest) and fiddle (Maren's theme), playing in alternating phrases rather than together — a walking-pace duet that suggests two people keeping their own rhythms alongside each other. At the deep spring in Act 2, a small entrance of wooden recorder (Torbin's theme) enters briefly, as if the quest itself is reminding the listener who sent them on this walk. The recorder fades before the scene ends. At the stone circle in Act 3, all three instruments drop out. A single low bowed bass holds a sustained note from the moment Aric sees the symbol on the seventh stone until he opens the wooden box. If he reads the letter at the stone, the bass continues under the reading; if he takes it back unread, the bass fades as he and Maren leave the clearing. There is no melodic resolution in Act 3; the piece ends on an open fifth.

### sound-design

The deep spring has a very particular sound layer: water trickling into the basin at a clear pitched frequency, ferns rustling softly, and underneath, the same healthy-woods whispering as Hollow's Rest — a forest that has not been corrupted, rendered in a sound palette that is noticeably different from the rest of the son's-era woods. Maren's hand entering the water has a small clean splash followed by a ring of ripples — audible because the basin's surface is otherwise glass-still. The stone circle's soundscape is the opposite: wind moving through a thinning treeline with a dry edge, the fungal growth on the fallen stones producing an occasional small crackle as temperature shifts, and a low subsonic hum near the standing stone that Aric feels before he hears. The wooden box, when Aric lifts it out of the leaf-mold, has a small grating sound against soil and a faint wet-wood creak. The letter inside is dry — it has been sealed inside the box, which is sealed inside a wax-dipped inner sleeve Kaelen made by hand. When Aric breaks the wax seal, there is a small dry crack and a faint scent of old candlewax that is not a sound but is in the scene. The reading of the letter itself has no background music and no ambience other than the wind. Kaelen's written words are read in Aric's voice (or Torbin's, if that choice was made) and nothing else shares the sound space with them.
