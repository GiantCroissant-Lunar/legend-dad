# Vanguard Checkpoint

## Overview

A Son-era main quest that forces Aric to confront the Iron Vanguard directly. The military faction has established a fortified checkpoint on the only maintained road between the lowlands and the Iron Peaks, blocking all civilian access to the mining region. Twenty years ago, the Vanguard was a professional but cooperative force — Kaelen dealt with them as respectful equals. In the intervening decades, the Vanguard has militarized aggressively, seizing control of the Iron Peaks mines under the justification of "geological security" and cutting off the communities that once depended on the mining trade. Aric must get past the checkpoint to reach the Iron Peaks tunnel system. How he does it reveals what kind of hero he will become — and whether he can avoid repeating his father's mistakes.

## Narrative Arc

**Act 1 — The Blockade.** Aric and Maren approach the checkpoint after leaving the Whispering Woods. The road narrows into a canyon pass where the Vanguard has built a timber-and-stone gatehouse flanked by watchtowers. Soldiers in iron-banded armor patrol the walls. A posted decree declares the Iron Peaks a restricted military zone — no civilians permitted without a command seal. Refugees and displaced miners camp in a makeshift settlement outside the checkpoint, unable to return to their homes or livelihoods. Aric learns from the refugees that the Vanguard seized the mines three years ago after a "resonance incident" killed a patrol squad underground.

**Act 2 — Three Paths.** Aric must choose his approach to the checkpoint. Each path is a self-contained sequence with its own challenges and consequences.

- **Diplomacy:** Aric requests an audience with the checkpoint commander, Captain Voss. He can invoke his father's name (Kaelen was known and respected by the old Vanguard), present evidence of the crystal corruption as a shared threat, or offer to serve as a civilian scout in exchange for passage. Voss is pragmatic but bound by orders from Vanguard high command — she can be convinced, but only if Aric offers something the Vanguard needs.

- **Stealth:** Maren knows the canyon terrain and identifies a goat trail that climbs above the checkpoint and reconnects with the road on the far side. The trail is narrow, exposed, and monitored by a roving Vanguard patrol. Aric and Maren must time their crossing, avoid detection, and navigate a crumbling cliff section. Success means bypassing the Vanguard entirely — but also missing information that Voss could have provided.

- **Force:** Aric can challenge the checkpoint guard to a formal duel for passage (a tradition the old Vanguard honored but the current command has largely abandoned) or create a distraction using the refugee camp's grievances to draw soldiers away from the gate. Direct assault against a fortified position is suicidal — the quest design makes this clear through environmental storytelling (arrow slits, murder holes, superior numbers).

**Act 3 — The Other Side.** Regardless of approach, Aric reaches the far side of the checkpoint and gets his first clear view of the Iron Peaks — a jagged mountain range with visible scarring from decades of mining, now threaded with the same violet crystal growths he saw in the Whispering Woods. The corruption is worse here: entire cliff faces shimmer with crystalline deposits. The road ahead leads to the mine entrance that Kaelen found barricaded twenty years ago. The barricade is gone now — replaced by a dark, open maw in the mountainside. Something has broken out.

## Objectives & Stakes

- **Primary objective:** Get past the Iron Vanguard checkpoint and reach the Iron Peaks road.
- **Secondary objective:** Learn what the Vanguard knows about the "resonance incident" that killed their patrol squad three years ago.
- **Optional objective:** Help the refugee miners with supplies or information (builds reputation, unlocks a refugee guide who knows the old mine tunnels for a later quest).
- **Stakes:** The checkpoint is the only practical route to the Iron Peaks. Without passage, Aric cannot reach the source of the crystal corruption. The broader stakes are political: the Vanguard's lockdown is strangling lowland communities that depend on Iron Peaks trade, and Aric's approach to the checkpoint sets the tone for his relationship with the most powerful military faction in the region. A cooperative Aric gains intelligence but owes the Vanguard a debt. A defiant Aric preserves independence but makes an enemy.

## Branching Points

1. **Kaelen's Name.** If Aric invokes his father's reputation during diplomacy, Captain Voss reacts with complicated respect — she served under a commander who knew Kaelen personally. This opens a dialogue branch where Voss reveals that the Vanguard found Kaelen's abandoned camp in the deep tunnels years ago, along with Iron Dawn driven into a stone wall as if marking a boundary. This information is exclusive to the diplomacy path.

2. **Maren's Risk.** On the stealth path, the crumbling cliff section forces a choice: Maren can lead (she is the better climber, but if she falls, Aric loses his only companion and guide) or Aric can lead (slower, riskier for him, but protects Maren). If Maren leads and the climb succeeds, their trust deepens. If Aric leads, Maren recognizes his protectiveness and warns him not to become his father — always shielding others from danger they can handle themselves.

3. **The Refugees.** Helping the refugee miners is optional but shapes the quest's moral texture. Aric can distribute supplies from his own pack (generous but leaves him under-equipped), promise to advocate for their return once the Peaks are safe (political, commits him to a future obligation), or recruit willing refugees as guides (practical, but puts civilians in danger). Ignoring the refugees entirely is possible but Maren comments on it critically.

4. **Captain Voss's Offer.** If diplomacy succeeds, Voss offers Aric a Vanguard communication token — a way to signal the checkpoint if he discovers anything underground. Accepting it means the Vanguard can track his position. Refusing it preserves operational secrecy but closes off potential Vanguard reinforcement in a later quest. Voss makes no threat either way; she simply presents it as a professional courtesy between people who want the same thing.

## Creative Prompts

### scene-art

16-bit pixel art, wide establishing shot of the canyon checkpoint. A narrow rocky pass between red-brown cliff walls, the road paved with worn flagstones leading to a heavy timber gatehouse with iron-reinforced doors. Two stone watchtowers flank the gate, each flying the Iron Vanguard banner — a black anvil on a steel-gray field. Soldiers in dark iron half-plate stand at the gate and walk the walls. In the foreground, the refugee camp sprawls across the roadside: patched canvas tents, a communal cook-fire with thin smoke rising, miners sitting on crates with the exhausted posture of people who have been waiting for weeks. Aric and Maren stand at the edge of the camp, studying the checkpoint from a distance. The canyon walls rise steeply on both sides, streaked with mineral deposits that catch the late afternoon sun in bands of rust and gold. The mood is tense standoff.

### music

Military march deconstructed and made uneasy. Opens with a snare drum cadence in strict 4/4 time — crisp, authoritative, the sound of an institution that believes in its own order. Over the march, a solo trumpet plays a minor-key melody that is technically precise but emotionally cold, representing the Vanguard's shift from protectors to occupiers. When the refugee camp comes into view, the march fades and is replaced by a solo acoustic guitar playing a tired, circular folk pattern — the same three chords repeating without resolution, evoking stagnation and waiting. During the stealth path, all melodic instruments drop out, leaving only ambient wind and a quiet sustained bass note that tightens with proximity to the patrol. During the diplomacy path, trumpet and guitar play simultaneously in different keys, creating controlled tension that resolves only if negotiation succeeds.

### sound-design

Canyon acoustics with natural reverb — sounds bounce between the cliff walls with a half-second delay, making distant voices unintelligible but present. The checkpoint gate has a mechanical quality: chains clinking against stone, the creak of a heavy crossbar, boots on wooden platforms overhead. Vanguard soldiers speak in clipped, professional cadences — not hostile but not welcoming, the vocal register of people following procedures. The refugee camp is a contrast: low murmured conversations, a child coughing, the scrape of a ladle against the bottom of a nearly empty pot, canvas flapping in the canyon draft. On the stealth path, the goat trail is defined by loose gravel crunching underfoot, wind gusting unpredictably, and the distant echo of patrol boots on stone below. The crumbling cliff section adds the sound of small rocks breaking loose and falling — each one a sharp crack followed by a long, rattling descent that makes the height viscerally real.
