# The Missing Patrol

## Overview

A side quest in Aric's era, set in and around Ashenford. Before Aric attempts the Vanguard Checkpoint (see quest of that name), he learns from the refugee camp outside Ashenford's southern gate that the Vanguard has an open case — a patrol of three soldiers that went into the Iron Peaks six months ago and never returned, and that the refugees believe the Vanguard has stopped looking for. The Wounded Sentry, one of three survivors of that patrol and the only one still alive, stands his watches at the southern gate with a crystalline arm that he does not explain. A refugee midwife asks Aric if he is going into the Peaks anyway — and if he is, would he look. The Vanguard will not officially sanction this. Captain Berin, asked privately at the depot, hands Aric a worn map and a small iron signal-token without a word.

## Narrative Arc

**Act 1 — The Refugee Camp's Ask.** Aric and Maren stop in Ashenford on their way to the Vanguard Checkpoint. Rather than entering the main garrison, they pass through the refugee camp at the southern gate and take shelter with a small group who recognize Maren's family name. An older midwife named Alva — whose granddaughter was one of the three missing soldiers — asks Aric, privately, whether he is going into the Peaks. If he is, she asks him to look for her granddaughter, Corporal Neska. She has a locket of Neska's with a small painted portrait inside. She is not asking him to bring Neska back. She knows what that would mean. She is asking for a name on a stone if a stone can be found.

**Act 2 — The Sentry's Watch.** On the way out of Ashenford, Aric encounters the Wounded Sentry at the southern gate post. The sentry does not challenge them; he is on watch and they have business the Vanguard is not formally aware of. Aric can ignore him, greet him and move on, or stop and speak with him. If he speaks — and it is a choice that costs time, because a sergeant is due to rotate the watch in about twelve minutes — the sentry answers questions flatly and briefly. He does not remember what happened. He remembers going in with Neska and the other two. He remembers being alone on a slope with a heavy arm. He remembers the smell underground — metallic, cold, and sweet. If Aric stays long enough to ask a fourth question, the sentry says, without prompting: "Neska went further in than I did. She was ahead when the sound started." The sound. Aric does not need to ask what sound.

**Act 3 — The Slope Above.** Following Berin's map and the sentry's half-remembered directions, Aric and Maren climb a goat trail off the main canyon road, above the Vanguard Checkpoint, to a rocky slope that overlooks a sealed tunnel entrance the Vanguard has not officially mapped. The slope is the place where the sentry was found. Aric searches. He finds two things. The first is a broken signal-horn, partly fused with pale violet crystal, that matches the style Ashenford's sentries carry. The second, twenty yards up the slope, is a small half-buried cairn of stones that Neska must have built herself before she went further in — a marker a trained soldier makes when she does not expect to come back. Under the cairn is a folded scrap of paper with three words in her handwriting: "Tell Alva sorry." Nothing more.

## Objectives & Stakes

- **Primary objective:** Find evidence of Corporal Neska's fate on the slope above the sealed tunnel and bring something back to Alva.
- **Secondary objective:** Speak with the Wounded Sentry long enough to get his account of the patrol's last known movements.
- **Optional objective:** Return the broken crystal-fused signal-horn to Berin at the depot — he will not officially accept it, but he will file it in a drawer where the Vanguard's case on the missing patrol can no longer claim "no evidence."
- **Stakes:** For Alva, this is the difference between not knowing and knowing. For Aric, this is the first concrete encounter with a human cost of the corruption that is not mediated by story or journal — a fellow soldier's last written words, left under a cairn. For the Vanguard, if Berin accepts the signal-horn, the official "patrol missing, presumed dead" classification gets a small true note appended to it that the institution has been avoiding.

## Branching Points

1. **Speaking with the Sentry.** Aric can skip past him (saves time; loses the fourth-question revelation about the sound), greet him and move on (polite, neutral), or stop for a full four-question conversation (costs twelve minutes, and the sergeant's watch rotation means Aric will be noticed loitering at the gate — Berin covers for him with a falsified logbook entry the sergeant does not bother to verify). The full conversation is the only way Aric learns what the sentry heard underground, which matters later.

2. **Maren's Question About the Arm.** During or after the conversation, Maren asks Aric, privately, what he thinks about the sentry's arm. Aric can say what Physician Wren told him (that she has seen crystal-fusion in two patients and both died within weeks — implying the sentry should already be dead and is not), speculate (that the arm is not growing, which makes it unlike Wren's patients), or deflect. His answer shapes whether he chooses, later, to try to bring the sentry back to Wren — a possibility that opens up only if he takes this moment seriously.

3. **Neska's Note.** Aric can bring the note back to Alva as it is (the honest choice), fold it into the locket and return it silently without showing her the three words (a kindness that deprives Alva of her granddaughter's actual last message), or keep the note for himself (a small private betrayal; Alva would not know, but Maren would, and Maren does not forgive it). Each choice has a follow-up scene at the refugee camp that colors Aric's standing there for the rest of the son's-era arc.

4. **The Signal-Horn to Berin.** If Aric brings the crystal-fused signal-horn to the depot, Berin takes it without speaking and files it in a drawer. Aric can then leave (the clean version) or ask Berin, directly, why the Vanguard stopped looking for the patrol. Berin will answer honestly — that the current command classified the tunnels as "non-recoverable" after the third body — and then ask, just as directly, whether Aric is going into the tunnels himself. Whatever Aric says, Berin notes it. The note goes into the same drawer.

## Creative Prompts

### scene-art

16-bit pixel art, three distinct scene compositions for this quest's three acts. Act 1 (refugee camp): a low timber-and-canvas shelter at the camp's edge, Alva sitting on an overturned crate with the locket open in her palm, Maren kneeling in front of her, Aric standing, listening. The camp's other tents are visible in the background as a blurred wash of browns and grays. Cold late-afternoon light. Act 2 (the gate): the Wounded Sentry at his post, half-silhouetted against the canyon's shadow, the crystalline arm catching the evening light with a faint violet glow that marks him out from everything else in the frame. Aric and Maren stand ten feet from him, Maren's hand resting on her bow but not drawing. Act 3 (the slope): a wind-scoured rocky slope above the tunnel entrance, the cairn visible as a small pile of stones in the foreground with a scrap of paper caught under the top stone. The sealed tunnel entrance is visible in the distance as a dark maw; pale violet light seeps from the cracks around its seal. The sky is the flat silver-gray of a day that cannot decide whether to rain. Aric is kneeling; Maren stands watch, looking down the slope.

### music

A three-movement piece that follows the three acts. Act 1 (the ask): a solo wooden flute in A minor plays a simple folk melody over a low hand-drum pulse, very quiet. The melody is borrowed from Thornwall's market theme from the father's era — one of the songs Alva used to sing at harvest. The flute plays it badly once, better the second time, and stops on an unresolved note. Act 2 (the gate): the flute is gone. A single sustained high crystal-synthesizer tone — the same one that is associated with the Wounded Sentry's theme — is the entire piece, held under quiet natural ambience. Act 3 (the slope): the flute returns, playing the same Act-1 melody, but now joined by a low bowed bass that provides a grounding drone in the parallel minor. When Aric uncovers Neska's note, the flute stops and the bass alone holds a single long note for twelve seconds as he reads. The note fades rather than ends.

### sound-design

The refugee camp has a specific soundscape — low voices, a child being hushed, a wooden ladle in a clay pot, canvas flapping in the canyon wind, a small fire popping at irregular intervals. It is a domestic soundscape that has been dragged outdoors and made temporary. At the southern gate, the Vanguard's presence is audible as a steady drum-beat of activity at a distance (boots, gate chain, officer calling something) that contrasts with the sentry's personal silence — his boots do not shift, his breathing is not audible, the crystalline arm produces a very faint high-frequency hum at the edge of perception. On the slope, the wind is the dominant sound layer — dry, pitched, cold — punctuated by small rockfalls from the ridge above. The cairn's paper has a small specific sound when Aric lifts the stone: the paper is stiff from weather but has not been rained on; it crackles once. Neska's three words have no sound effect. They are read in complete silence. The note is placed back under the stone (if Aric does so) with a small dry sound, and the wind picks back up on the exhale.
