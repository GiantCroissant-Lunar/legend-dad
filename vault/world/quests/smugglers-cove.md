# Smuggler's Cove

## Overview

A side quest in Aric's era, set in Saltmere Port. After the Whispering Woods investigation, Aric and Maren are running low on medicines that Thornwall's herb shop can no longer supply — the specific sea-salt and kelp-derived tinctures that Physician Wren has told them work against early-stage crystal corruption are only available on the coast, and the only coastal port still trading is Saltmere. Cael Vire is willing to sell, but the Vanguard has begun inspecting all official port outflow, and the inspection logs would flag anyone carrying coastal medicines toward the Peaks. Cael offers Aric an alternative: a small hidden cove south of the harbor where her private trade moves, and where Aric can meet a boat that will not be logged. The catch is that the cove is watched, sometimes, by Vanguard patrols on the coast road — and the watcher on duty the night of the trade is the one variable Cael cannot control.

## Narrative Arc

**Act 1 — The Ask.** Aric and Maren arrive at the Saltscale Inn two days after the events of "Whispers in the Woods" on Physician Wren's list of needed supplies. Brine recognizes Aric's eyes before he introduces himself; he does not comment. Cael joins them at the back table after supper. She has been expecting them. The story she tells is clean: there is a stretch of coast, half an hour south, where she can arrange a meeting with a boat from a coastal-plain physician's guild. The boat will bring what is needed. Aric will meet it. No logs, no money changes hands in Saltmere proper, and Cael's only payment is an old favor Aric's father once did her twenty years ago (see "Iron in the Tide"). Aric accepts. Maren watches Cael as she tells the story and does not trust a word of it. Cael notices. She does not mind.

**Act 2 — The Coast Road.** Aric and Maren walk the coast road south in the late afternoon. The road is lightly patrolled but not aggressively so. Maren is attentive; she spots a boot-print too fresh to be from the morning patrol and marks it. Halfway to the cove, they find a dead seabird crusted with pale mineral residue on its feathers — the first physical evidence Aric has seen of the corruption reaching the coast. The cove itself is a narrow crescent of shingle beach at the base of a low chalk cliff, screened from the road by a stand of wind-bent pines. A rowboat is already waiting, pulled up on the shingle, with two figures standing beside it and a third sitting atop the cliff keeping watch.

**Act 3 — The Meeting.** The trade goes as Cael described it: the boat's pilot is a physician's apprentice named Ness, the watcher atop the cliff is Scavenger Mott (working a favor for Cael in exchange for information about the Vanguard's patrol schedule), and the medicines are wrapped in oilcloth and ready. Aric hands over the payment (a small sealed letter from Torbin to a coastal physician — Ness's teacher — that Torbin asked him to deliver), and Ness hands over the medicines. The trade is clean. What is not clean is the second sound Mott signals from the cliff: a Vanguard patrol has come up the coast road twenty minutes earlier than expected. The rowboat must leave now. Aric and Maren must get off the beach and back to the road before the patrol rounds the headland. How they extract determines whether this is a simple supply run or a story the Vanguard eventually hears.

## Objectives & Stakes

- **Primary objective:** Meet the rowboat at the hidden cove and complete the medicine trade for Hollow's Rest and Thornwall.
- **Secondary objective:** Deliver Torbin's sealed letter to Ness for her teacher without breaking the seal or reading it.
- **Optional objective:** Ask Cael — once, directly — about her son, whose fishing boat was found with a crystalline film on its hull two years ago. She will not answer, but she will note that Aric asked.
- **Stakes:** Hollow's Rest needs the medicines to survive the winter; Thornwall's herb supply has already failed for three patients this season. The Vanguard patrol's early arrival is the practical stakes: if Aric or Maren are seen on the beach, the cove is burned as a trade route and Cael's private network loses one of its cleaner arrangements.

## Branching Points

1. **Trust Cael.** In Act 1, Maren asks Aric privately, at the inn upstairs, whether he is going to trust the harbormaster who knew his father. Aric can say yes (committing to the full trade as described), yes-but-prepared (the same trade, but with Maren watching Cael's people and ready to extract if things go wrong — a subtle cost to Cael's trust), or no and propose a counter-plan (a different trade route that Maren can arrange through her forester contacts — slower, harder, but independent of Cael). Each choice shifts who owes whom a favor at the quest's end.

2. **Maren's Boot-Print.** When Maren spots the out-of-schedule boot-print on the coast road, she can share the observation with Aric immediately (who then uses it to decide whether to proceed) or keep it to herself and stay alert (faster progress, but Aric walks in less informed). Sharing lets Aric choose whether to abort; keeping it honors Maren's judgment but takes the choice away from him. There is a small rising theme in Maren's character about when she defers to him and when she does not.

3. **The Seal.** Torbin's sealed letter is not for Aric to read. He can deliver it unopened (the obvious choice — and the right one), or he can open it before the trade (a small but real betrayal of Torbin's trust, unlocking a piece of late-game backstory early). If opened, the letter reveals that the coastal physician's guild is one of three surviving fragments of the Starlight Academy's old medical school, and that Torbin has been exchanging careful letters with them about the crystal corruption for two years — not as refugee correspondence, but as research. This information becomes available to Aric later anyway; opening it here costs him Torbin's trust when Torbin realizes the seal is broken on his next supply run.

4. **The Extraction.** When the Vanguard patrol arrives early, Aric and Maren can go up the cliff path (fast, exposed, relies on Mott's cover — Mott is willing but it costs him his cover for the next trade), hide on the beach and let the patrol pass (slow, low-visibility, requires good rolls, and Ness's rowboat may have to abort its return journey), or approach the patrol directly with a cover story (risky, depends on Aric's social skill, and if it fails, the cove is burned). Each extraction leaves a different trace on the map of what Cael and the Vanguard will know about each other after tonight.

## Creative Prompts

### scene-art

16-bit pixel art, side-scroll perspective for the cove scene (distinct from the top-down port view). A narrow crescent of pale shingle beach at the base of a low chalk cliff, with wind-bent pines growing at odd angles along the cliff edge. A small rowboat pulled up on the shingle, its oars shipped, a single lantern at its prow masked with a cloth to dim the light to a glow. Two figures beside the boat — Ness (a thin woman in a physician's apprentice cloak, hood back, dark hair wind-whipped) and a silent boatman keeping the vessel steady. Atop the cliff, a small silhouette: Mott, crouched, watching the road. Aric and Maren walk along the shingle toward the boat, their boots leaving brief prints in the wet stones. The sea beyond is a dark teal-gray, low waves breaking quietly. Sky is indigo with a low band of pink at the horizon — dusk, twenty minutes past sunset. The only warm light is Ness's masked lantern and the small distant flicker of the Saltmere harbor's landward beacon. Mood: quiet, cold, deliberate.

### music

Solo classical guitar in E minor, played fingerstyle, very quiet. The melody is a variation on the Saltmere Port theme from the son's era, but pared down to the fewest notes that still identify the tune — the sound of a harbor remembered rather than heard. In the walk down the coast road, a low bowed bass adds a slow heartbeat underneath, suggesting the subterranean tremor that Kaelen felt in "Iron in the Tide" — now long-established and settled into the land. At the moment of the trade, the guitar plays a short ornament that is not quite a resolution. When Mott signals the incoming patrol, the guitar stops mid-phrase, replaced by silence and the sound of wind. Whether the theme resumes is determined by the extraction choice: the clean extractions let it resume; the bad ones end on silence and the sound of boots on shingle.

### sound-design

Quiet coastal night as the dominant bed — very small waves breaking at long intervals, wind through the wind-bent pines (a dry, papery rustle), a distant buoy bell from the harbor a half-mile away, the occasional gull call that sounds wrong at night. Inside the cove, the shingle crunches differently under Aric and Maren's boots than under Ness's or the boatman's — Ness has been walking on this kind of stone her whole life and barely makes a sound. Torbin's letter, when Aric breaks its seal (if he does), has a small specific sound — the snap of wax and a faint creak of old parchment — that is foregrounded and carries a small amount of shame in the mix (slight pitch waver, a tiny reverb that is not in the room). When the Vanguard patrol is signaled, the cove's ambience compresses: the waves seem quieter, the wind sharper, and the listener hears their own heartbeat as a low pulse in the mix. The patrol's boots on the coast road above are not heard directly; they are heard as a change in the wind's texture when Mott's whistle signals their arrival — a whistle that is itself a forester's bird-call imitation, not a military signal.
