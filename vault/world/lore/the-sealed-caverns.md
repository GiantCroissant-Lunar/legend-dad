# The Sealed Caverns

## Overview

Deep beneath the [[Iron Peaks]], a network of natural caverns extends far below the reach of the old mines. Two generations ago, miners broke through the lowest tunnel wall and discovered a vast underground chamber filled with a crystalline substance that hummed audibly, emitted faint violet light, and killed three workers within days of exposure. The mining guild sealed the breach with stone and mortar, abandoned the mines entirely, and the surviving miners' families relocated south to found [[Thornwall]]. What lies sealed in those caverns has been leaking through the cracks ever since — slowly, imperceptibly at first, but with increasing force. The tremors that drove [[Kaelen]] north and that now threaten Aric's world originate here.

## Details

The crystalline substance found in the caverns does not match any known mineral. Miners who survived initial contact described it as warm to the touch, geometrically perfect in its growth patterns, and responsive to sound — striking a crystal with a pick caused the entire chamber to resonate, producing a tone felt in the chest rather than heard with the ears. The three miners who died showed similar symptoms: progressive disorientation, vivid nightmares of places they had never visited, and a final stage in which they spoke in languages no one recognized before falling silent permanently.

The mining guild's chief engineer, a woman named Thessaly, documented the breach in a sealed report before the mines were abandoned. Her account describes the cavern as "alive in a way stone should not be" and notes that the crystals appeared to be growing — the breach wall was partially covered in new crystal growth within the two days between discovery and sealing. Thessaly recommended the breach be filled with rubble and mortared shut, which was done. She also recommended that no one dig below the five-hundred-foot level of the Iron Peaks again. This recommendation was followed until the mining guild dissolved entirely.

The seal held for approximately forty years without incident. The first sign of degradation came during Kaelen's era — faint vibrations in the upper mine tunnels, occasional reports of a humming sound near the sealed level, and a subtle change in the water table that dried several wells in Thornwall. Kaelen investigated the upper mines and found hairline cracks in the seal mortar, through which faint purple light was visible. This discovery, combined with the first tremor, convinced him that the caverns were not merely a geological curiosity but an active and growing threat.

In Aric's era, the degradation has accelerated dramatically. The seal has fractured in multiple places. Crystal growth has pushed through the cracks and now extends into the lower mine tunnels. The tremors have increased in frequency and intensity. Creatures not seen on the surface — eyeless, crystalline-armored things adapted to total darkness — have begun emerging from the deep tunnels. The energy leaking from the caverns is visibly affecting the surrounding landscape: the [[Whispering Woods]] are dying, sinkholes follow ancient underground channels, and the iron-bark pines nearest the peaks have petrified.

The nature of the sealed energy remains unknown. Whether it is a natural geological phenomenon, a remnant of an ancient civilization, or something deliberately imprisoned is one of the central mysteries of the game. The Starweavers' master charts may contain the answer — if anyone can reach them.

## Cultural Significance

In Thornwall, the sealed caverns occupy a space between local history and folk legend. The founding families remember the truth: their grandparents sealed something dangerous and built a town far enough away to feel safe. But over two generations, the story has softened. Children are told the mines were abandoned because the ore ran out. The caverns are mentioned as a curiosity, not a threat. Only the village elders and a few old families still take the sealed caverns seriously.

Kaelen's departure — which he justified by citing the caverns as a growing danger — was met with skepticism by most of Thornwall. The tremors were too faint for ordinary people to notice, and Kaelen's warnings sounded like excuses for wanderlust. This skepticism became the dominant narrative: the hero left because he couldn't sit still, not because the mountains were waking up. Aric grew up hearing this version.

The returning tremors in Aric's era have forced Thornwall to reconsider. The elders who dismissed Kaelen's warnings now face the reality he tried to describe. The sealed caverns have gone from a forgotten footnote to an existential threat within a single generation — a reminder that buried dangers do not stay buried, and that the decisions of one generation become the crises of the next.
