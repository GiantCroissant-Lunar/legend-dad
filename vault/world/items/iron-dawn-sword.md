# Iron Dawn

## Overview

Kaelen's broadsword, forged from iron ore mined in the upper tunnels of the Iron Peaks. The blade is plain but well-balanced — a working weapon, not a ceremonial one. It was a gift from the Thornwall militia when Kaelen first set out on his journey. Over the years, the sword has become a symbol of Kaelen's legend. In Aric's era, Iron Dawn is missing — Kaelen took it with him. Finding the sword (or what remains of it) is a key objective in the son's storyline, as it proves Kaelen reached his destination.

## Lore & Origin

Forged by Thornwall's blacksmith, Garrett, from high-grade iron ore Kaelen's father had stockpiled from his mining days. The sword was intended as a militia-issue weapon but Garrett spent extra time on the balance and edge, sensing Kaelen would need it. The name "Iron Dawn" was given by village children who watched Kaelen practice at sunrise, the blade catching the first light. Kaelen kept the name, amused. The sword has no magical properties — its significance is entirely narrative and sentimental.

## Purpose & Usage

In Kaelen's era, Iron Dawn is his primary weapon — a reliable broadsword with no special abilities beyond solid craftsmanship. It serves as a visual identifier in cutscenes and character art. In Aric's era, the sword is a quest objective: finding it proves Kaelen's path. When Aric eventually discovers Iron Dawn, it is damaged — the blade is cracked and corroded from exposure to the sealed cavern's crystal energy. It can be reforged (with new materials) into a different weapon that inherits its legacy but reflects Aric's fighting style — shorter, lighter, adapted for a tracker rather than a swordsman.

## Creative Prompts

### item-art

16-bit pixel art, inventory icon (16x16 and 32x32 variants). Father's era: a straight-bladed broadsword with a simple iron crossguard and leather-wrapped grip. The blade is polished steel-gray with a slight blue tint from the Iron Peaks ore. The pommel is a plain iron ball. No ornamentation — this is a tool, not a trophy. The scabbard is brown leather with iron fittings. Son's era (damaged): the same sword but with visible cracks along the blade, a purple-tinged corrosion pattern where crystal energy has eaten the metal, the leather grip is brittle and frayed, the crossguard is bent. Son's era (reforged): shorter blade, curved slightly, with new dark steel replacing the corroded sections — a visual hybrid of the old sword and new material.

### sound-effect

Drawing the sword: a clear, ringing steel-on-leather sound — the classic unsheathe. Swinging: a medium-weight whoosh with a metallic edge, not heavy or sluggish. Impact on enemy: a solid clang with a brief resonant ring. Impact on stone: a sharper, shorter clang with spark sound. Sheathing: the reverse of drawing — steel sliding into leather with a soft click at the end. Reforged variant: all sounds gain a subtle lower harmonic, giving the weapon a deeper voice.
