# Starweaver Lens

## Overview

A palm-sized disc of polished crystal set in a brass frame, used by the Academy's scholars to read celestial patterns invisible to the naked eye. The lens was standard equipment for Starweavers in the Academy's golden age; now only three remain, all held by Elder Aldric. He lends one to Sera for her studies — an act of trust that surprises the other scholars. The lens reveals hidden star-chart inscriptions on the Academy's walls and can be used to decipher the carved stones found in the Whispering Woods.

## Lore & Origin

The lenses were crafted by the Academy's founders using techniques now lost. The crystal is not glass — it's a naturally occurring mineral found only in the deep caves beneath the Academy's cliff. The founders called it "starstone" and believed it was condensed starlight that had seeped into the earth over millennia. Modern scholars dismiss this as myth, but the lenses genuinely reveal patterns in light that are otherwise invisible. Aldric suspects the crystal is related to the substance found in the Iron Peaks sealed caverns — a connection he has not shared with anyone.

## Purpose & Usage

The Starweaver Lens functions as a key item that unlocks hidden information throughout the game. When equipped, it reveals: hidden inscriptions on Academy walls, readable star-chart patterns in the night sky, the true nature of the carved stones in the Whispering Woods, and faint energy trails near the Iron Peaks fissures. It is essential for solving several puzzle sequences. In the son's era, Aric can obtain a cracked lens from the Academy's archives — it works intermittently, showing fragments of what the full lens reveals.

## Creative Prompts

### item-art

16-bit pixel art, inventory icon (16x16 and 32x32 variants). A circular disc of translucent pale blue crystal, set in an ornate brass frame with small gear-like teeth along the edge (suggesting it can be rotated). The crystal has a faint internal glow — not bright, but visible against dark backgrounds. The brass frame has tiny star symbols engraved around the rim. A short chain loop for wearing around the neck. When active (in-game use): the lens emits a soft cone of blue-white light, and hidden elements in the environment become visible within that cone as glowing outlines.

### sound-effect

Equipping the lens: a crystalline chime ascending three notes (C-E-G), followed by a subtle sustained hum that persists while the lens is active. Revealing a hidden inscription: a shimmering wash of high-frequency tones, like glass being rubbed, resolving into a clear bell tone. Deactivating: the hum fades, and a single descending note (glass harmonica quality) signals return to normal vision.
