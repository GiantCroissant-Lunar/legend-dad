# Kaelen's Journal

## Overview

A worn leather journal filled with Kaelen's handwriting — sketches of locations, notes on creatures encountered, half-formed theories about the tremors beneath the Iron Peaks, and personal reflections he never shared with anyone. Aric discovers it hidden in the militia barracks at age twelve. The journal serves as the primary link between the two timelines: its entries give Aric (and the player) clues about where Kaelen went and what he found, but the entries are incomplete, out of order, and sometimes contradicted by what Aric discovers firsthand.

## Lore & Origin

Kaelen started the journal when he first left Thornwall. He wrote in it irregularly — sometimes daily, sometimes with months-long gaps. The journal was left behind in the barracks during one of Kaelen's brief returns to Thornwall (he carried it back to leave for Aric, though he never told anyone this directly). The leather cover bears a stamped iron clasp shaped like the Thornwall militia's emblem. Several pages have been torn out — Kaelen removed entries he didn't want anyone to read.

## Purpose & Usage

The journal functions as a game mechanic and narrative device. When Aric reaches a location his father visited, the journal unlocks the corresponding entry, revealing Kaelen's observations. Some entries contain sketches that serve as map hints. Others contain warnings that are now twenty years out of date. The torn-out pages are a mystery thread — Aric can find the missing pages hidden in locations Kaelen visited, revealing the parts of the story Kaelen tried to hide. The journal also unlocks father's footprints on the world map (narratively gated — Aric sees his father's path only at locations mentioned in the journal).

16-bit pixel art, inventory icon (16x16 and 32x32 variants). Worn brown leather cover with visible stitching along the spine. Some pages have visible tears where entries were removed.

Opening the journal: soft leather creak, then the whisper of pages turning. Unlocking a new entry: a warm chime (mid-range bell tone) followed by Kaelen's theme guitar motif played on a music box — tinny, distant, like a memory. Discovering a torn page: a lower chime with a subtle discordant undertone, followed by the sound of paper being carefully unfolded.
