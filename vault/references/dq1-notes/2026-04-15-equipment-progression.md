| # | Weapon | Attack Bonus | Price (G) | Shop Location |

| 1 | Bamboo Pole | +2 | 10 | Brecconary |

| 4 | Hand Axe | +15 | 560 | Cantlin |
| 5 | Broad Sword | +20 | 1,500 | Rimuldar |
| 6 | Flame Sword (Fire Blade) | +28 | 9,800 | Rimuldar (or found) |

**Key design insight:** Only 7 weapons in the entire game. Each upgrade is a MAJOR power spike — the jump from +20 to +28 to +40 is dramatic. The Flame Sword can also be used as a battle item to cast Sizz for free fire damage, giving it dual utility.

| 7 | **Erdrick's Armour** | +28 | — (Damdara ruin, guarded) |

**Erdrick's Armour is loaded with bonuses:**
- +28 Defense
- Reduces magic/breath damage by 33%
- Complete immunity to enemy Fizzle (Stopspell)
- Protection from toxic bogs and damage barriers
- Regenerates 1 HP per step walked on the overworld
- The HP regen makes it THE exploration tool — turns the entire overworld into a healing zone

## Economy & Grinding

- **Starting gold:** 0 (you start with nothing)
- **Gold earned from Slimes:** ~1-2G each
- **Copper Sword (first upgrade):** 180G — requires ~90-180 early battles
- **Flame Sword:** 9,800G — massive grind investment (or skip it, find Erdrick's Sword)
- **Key design:** You can SKIP buying the Flame Sword entirely by going straight for Erdrick's Sword — this is a meaningful player choice (spend 9,800G on safety vs. take the harder path to get the best weapon free)

## Design Patterns for Legend Dad

1. **Small item pool, big impact:** 7 weapons, 7 armors, 2 shields = ~16 total equips. Each one matters. Contrast with modern RPGs that have hundreds of forgettable items.

2. **Legendary gear as quest objectives:** Erdrick's set (sword, armor, token) isn't bought — it's FOUND through exploration and puzzle-solving. The best items are rewards for adventure, not grind.

3. **Multi-functional equipment:** Flame Sword = weapon + free spell. Erdrick's Armour = defense + magic resist + terrain immunity + HP regen. Items do MORE than their primary stat.

4. **Equipment as progression gates:** Areas are survivable or not based on your gear. The shop prices create natural "grind walls" that gate progression alongside leveling.

5. **The Gold economy IS the difficulty:** Getting 9,800G for the Flame Sword means hours of grinding. The game asks: do you grind for gear, or take risks exploring further with weaker equipment?

6. **"Found vs. Bought" design tension:** Shop items are safe but expensive. Found items (Erdrick's set) are free but require solving puzzles and surviving dangerous areas. Both paths are valid.

7. **HP regen on legendary armor:** A brilliant design choice — the ultimate armor doesn't just protect you in combat, it transforms the entire overworld experience by removing the attrition factor. The game gets EASIER near the end, which feels rewarding.
