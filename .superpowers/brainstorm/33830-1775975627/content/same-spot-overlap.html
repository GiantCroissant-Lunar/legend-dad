When Father & Son Are at the Same Location

The world map shows overlapping markers. What special gameplay unlocks?

🔗

A. Temporal Resonance — Real-Time Cause & Effect

Puzzle Signature Mechanic

When both characters are at the same location, their views become linked. Father's actions in the past update the son's view in real-time. Move a boulder as the father — watch the path open in the son's view instantly. This is the tightest version of your cause-and-effect loop.

B. Echo Battle — Cross-Timeline Combat

Combat Signature Mechanic

Father fights a powerful enemy at a location. In the son's era, the same enemy (or its descendant) exists — but it still carries scars from the father's battle. When both are at the same spot, combat becomes linked: father's attacks in the past create lasting weaknesses the son can exploit in the future.

C. Timeline Convergence — The Views Merge

Story Puzzle Signature Mechanic

At key story moments when both characters are at the same sacred location, the two windows physically merge into one larger view — father and son occupy the same space across time. They can't see each other, but their actions interleave. The son steps on a floor plate that the father needs to unlock a door. The father lights a torch that illuminates the son's dark room.

D. Temporal Dungeon — Two-Era Linked Dungeon

Puzzle Combat

When both are at the same dungeon, it becomes a two-era linked dungeon. Same floor plan, different states. Father's dungeon is intact with traps and guards; son's is ruined but has collapsed paths. The player must switch between them to solve rooms: father opens water gates, son walks on the drained floor. Father defeats a guard blocking a lever, son uses the lever 20 years later.

These aren't mutually exclusive

  • Resonance (A) can be the default mechanic when both are at any shared location
  • Echo Battle (B) can be how boss fights work at shared locations
  • Convergence (C) can be reserved for 3-4 major story moments (rare, impactful)
  • Temporal Dungeon (D) can be the structure for shared-location dungeons
  • Normal locations use A. Bosses use A+B. Story climaxes use C. Dungeons use A+D.
