How should the two timelines share the screen?

The active era is where the player controls movement and actions. The inactive era is a live reference view.

A. Side by Side (50/50)

Left = father's era, right = son's era. Equal screen space. Both always visible. Classic split-screen feel. Works well on wide monitors, may feel cramped on narrow screens.

B. Main + Sidebar (70/30)

Active era gets most screen space. Inactive era is a smaller reference panel on the side. The sidebar shows the same location in the other timeline. When you switch, they swap sizes with a transition.

C. Fullscreen + Picture-in-Picture

Active era is fullscreen. The other timeline appears as a PiP overlay in the corner (like a minimap). Player can toggle PiP position or expand it. Maximizes gameplay space while keeping the other era visible.

D. Top/Bottom Stack

Father era on top, son era on bottom (or vice versa). Works better on portrait/narrow screens. Evokes the "two worlds" feel vertically. Could feel like looking through time layers.

Things to consider

  • Web target: Browser windows vary wildly in aspect ratio. Responsive layout matters.
  • Pixel art: If you go pixel art style, integer scaling is important — 50/50 split works cleanly with tile-based rendering.
  • Switch animation: When the player toggles active era, the transition can reinforce the "time shift" feel (ripple, fade, slide).
  • Cris Tales reference: That game used a single screen with past/present/future shown as vertical slices of the same scene. That's a fifth option if you want the most visually striking approach, but it's the hardest to implement with tile-based maps.
